

1) Description of Cheese: Buxton Blue, a modern creamery blue cheese, is a cousin of the famous Blue Stilton. It is made from cow's milk and has a Protected Designation of Origin (PDO), ensuring its authenticity and quality. Originating from Buxton, Derbyshire in England, this cheese has a rich history and is a proud representation of the United Kingdom's cheese-making tradition.

2) Description of Product: Buxton Blue has a unique appearance with thin blue veins running through its crumbly body. Its deep russet color adds to its appeal and the interior is a beautiful pale orange. Made with 45% fat content, this soft artisan cheese is coated with a natural rind. The cheese is made using only the finest cow's milk and traditional rennet, ensuring a pure and authentic taste.

3) Description of Process: Buxton Blue is carefully prepared and aged to perfection. The cheese is made using milk sourced from Nottinghamshire, Derbyshire, and Staffordshire, giving it a distinct flavor. It is then aged for the perfect amount of time, allowing the flavors to develop and the blue veins to form. This process results in a delicious and high-quality cheese.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: Buxton Blue has a unique aroma with hints of dark chocolate and burnt onions. Its flavor is rich and creamy with a perfect balance of tanginess from the blue veins. The taste is comparable to Shropshire Blue, making it a must-try for cheese lovers.

5) Serving Suggestions: For the best experience, serve Buxton Blue at room temperature. Its crumbly texture makes it perfect for crumbling over salads or pasta dishes. It pairs well with fruits like apples and pears, and also goes perfectly with a glass of red wine.

6) Quality Assurance: Buxton Blue has received numerous accolades and awards for its exceptional taste and quality. It has been quoted as a "must-try" cheese by renowned cheese experts and has passed strict quality tests. With its PDO status and rich history, Buxton Blue is a cheese that can be trusted for its authenticity and superior taste.
