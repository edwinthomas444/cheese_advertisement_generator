
1) Description of Cheese:
Introducing the award-winning Single Gloucester cheese, crafted by skilled artisans using traditional methods. This cheese is made with full cream milk from the morning milking and skimmed milk from the evening milking, sourced from the picturesque Gloucestershire countryside. Its origins can be traced back to the 16th century, making it a true taste of history.

2) Description of Product:
Single Gloucester cheese is known for its unique characteristics - a light texture and perfectly balanced taste. It comes in three sizes - a whole cheese weighing 3.4kg, a medium size of 1.5kg, and a smaller size of 0.34kg. Each cheese is coated in a natural rind, giving it a rustic appearance. Only unpasteurised milk and vegetarian rennet are used in the production of this cheese, making it suitable for vegetarians.

3) Description of Process:
Our cheese-making process begins with the careful selection of the freshest milk, which is then left to naturally curdle. The curds are then hand-ladled into traditional moulds and aged for a minimum of three weeks, allowing the flavours to develop and mature. This meticulous process ensures the highest quality cheese with a distinctive taste.

4) Description of Smell and Taste:
The Real Cheese Companion describes Single Gloucester as having a cool, clean tang and grassy sub-flavours, making it a delight for the senses. Its aroma is rich and earthy, with hints of fresh herbs. The taste is creamy and buttery, with a subtle tang that lingers on the palate.

5) Serving Suggestions:
For the best experience, we recommend serving Single Gloucester cheese at room temperature. Its natural rind adds a rustic touch to any cheese board. Pair it with crusty bread, crackers, or fresh fruits for a delicious snack. This cheese also pairs perfectly with a crisp white wine or a fruity red, enhancing its flavours.

6) Quality Assurance:
Our commitment to quality is evident in the numerous awards and accolades Single Gloucester has received. It holds a Protected Designation of Origin (PDO) status, ensuring that it is made using traditional methods and only in the Gloucestershire region.
