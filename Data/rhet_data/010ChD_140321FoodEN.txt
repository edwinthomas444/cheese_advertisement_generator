

1) Description of Cheese: Swaledale Cheese, handmade by the Reed family in Richmond, Yorkshire. This cheese has a rich history, dating back to the 11th century when Cistercian monks first brought cheese making techniques to the Yorkshire dales. Swaledale cheese is a cherished treasure of the dales, with a Protected Designation of Origin (PDO) status.

2) Description of Product: This cheese is a hard pressed, yet very moist cow's milk cheese. It is made using locally sourced milk and vegetarian rennet. The cheese has a traditional blue-grey rind and is available in a 1kg size (approx). Its texture is soft and moist, with a creamy color and a slight tang.

3) Description of Process: Swaledale cheese is prepared using traditional methods and aged for 4-6 weeks. The cheese is handmade and follows a farmhouse recipe, passed down from the Cistercian monks. The cheese is carefully crafted to maintain its high quality and unique taste.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: Swaledale cheese has a mild and smooth taste, with a slight tang. Its open texture is soft and moist, making it easy to slice and enjoy. The cheese has a creamy color and a rich, savory aroma that will tantalize your taste buds.

5) Serving Suggestions: For the best experience, it is recommended to serve Swaledale cheese at room temperature. This cheese pairs perfectly with a dry Riesling, enhancing its flavors. It can also be used as a cooking cheese, substituting Reblochon in the classic French dish, Tartiflette au Reblochon.

6) Quality Assurance: Swaledale cheese has received numerous accolades and awards, including its PDO status. This recognition ensures the quality and authenticity of the cheese, safeguarding its production for years to come. As the Reed family says, "Cheese and wine are a match made in heaven" and Swaledale cheese is no exception. Try it for yourself and experience the taste of tradition and quality.
