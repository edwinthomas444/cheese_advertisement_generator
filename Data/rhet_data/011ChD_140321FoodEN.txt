
1) Description of Cheese: Swaledale Cheese, made by the Reed family in Richmond, is a traditional, handmade, hard pressed cow's milk cheese with a Protected Designation of Origin (PDO) status. Its origins can be traced back to the 11th century, when Cistercian monks brought cheese making techniques from Normandy to the Yorkshire dales.

2) Description of Product: Swaledale Cheese is a 2.5kg (approx) wheel with a natural blue-grey rind. It has a mild and smooth flavor with a slight tang, and is creamy in color with a soft, moist open texture. It is made using locally sourced milk and vegetarian rennet.

3) Description of Process: Swaledale Cheese is prepared using traditional methods passed down from the Cistercian monks. It is aged for 4-6 weeks, during which time it develops its unique flavor and texture. The cheese is carefully monitored and turned regularly to ensure its quality.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: Swaledale Cheese has a distinct aroma that is both tangy and creamy. Its flavor is mild and smooth with a slight tang, and its texture is soft and moist. The cheese has a creamy color with a blue-grey rind, adding to its visual appeal.

5) Serving Suggestions: Swaledale Cheese is best enjoyed at room temperature, allowing its flavors to fully develop. It can be presented on a cheese board with crackers, fruits, and nuts. For a perfect pairing, we recommend a dry Riesling. Swaledale Cheese is also a fantastic cooking cheese, perfect for substituting Reblochon in the classic French cheese stew Tartiflette au Reblochon.

6) Quality Assurance: Swaledale Cheese is recognized worldwide for its quality and is accredited with a PDO status. It has received numerous awards and accolades, including quotes from satisfied customers and quality tests that prove its excellence. Our cheese is a cherished treasure of the Yorkshire dales, and we take great pride in ensuring its consistent quality for all to enjoy.
