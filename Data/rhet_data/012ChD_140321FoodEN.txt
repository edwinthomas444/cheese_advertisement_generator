

1) Description of Cheese:
Introducing Traditional Swaledale Cheese, a beloved treasure of the Yorkshire Dales. This PDO (Protected Designation of Origin) cheese is handcrafted by the Reed family in Richmond, using locally sourced milk and vegetarian rennet. With a history dating back to the 11th century, when Cistercian monks brought cheese making techniques from Normandy, Swaledale cheese is a true taste of tradition and provenance.

2) Description of Product:
This hard-pressed, cows milk cheese is coated in a natural colored wax, giving it a unique appearance. Weighing in at 2.5Kg (approx), it has a mild and smooth taste with a slight tang, and a soft, moist open texture. Made in the style of a farmhouse cheese, it is available throughout the year due to the readiness of Swaledale cows milk.

3) Description of Process:
The Swaledale recipe and methods have been passed down for generations and are protected by PDO status. Each batch of cheese is carefully handcrafted and aged for 4-6 weeks, ensuring the highest quality and consistency in every bite.

4) Description of Smell and Taste:
Upon opening the wax coating, you'll be greeted with a delightful aroma of creamy goodness. The taste is mild and smooth, with a slight tang that adds a depth of flavor. The creamy color and soft, moist texture make it a truly indulgent experience.

5) Serving Suggestions:
For the ultimate cheese and wine pairing, we recommend a dry Riesling to complement the flavors of Swaledale cheese. It also makes for a fantastic cooking cheese, perfect for substituting in classic French dishes like Tartiflette au Reblochon. Serve at room temperature for the best taste and texture, and impress your guests with this unique and delicious cheese.

6) Quality Assurance:
Don't just take our word for it, Swaledale cheese has received numerous awards and accolades, including PDO status, which recognizes its authenticity and quality. As one of the most cherished treasures of the Yorkshire Dales, you can trust that each bite of Swaledale cheese is a true testament to its rich history and superior taste. Try it for yourself and taste the tradition of Swaledale.
