

1) Description of Cheese:
Introducing Old Peculier Swaledale Cheese, crafted by the Reed family in Richmond since 1987. This handmade, farmhouse cheese has a rich history, dating back to the 11th century when Cistercian monks brought cheese-making techniques to the Yorkshire dales. Today, Swaledale cheese is a cherished treasure with Protected Designation of Origin (PDO) status, ensuring its quality and authenticity.

2) Description of Product:
Old Peculier Swaledale Cheese is a hard-pressed, yet moist, cows milk cheese with a creamy yellow color and open texture. Each pound of curd is infused with four fluid ounces of Theakstons Old Peculier ale, giving it a unique and distinct beer flavor. This 220g cheese is coated in black wax, preserving its freshness and flavor. It is made using locally sourced milk and vegetarian rennet.

3) Description of Process:
The Reed family follows the traditional cheese-making process, ensuring the highest quality and taste. The cheese is prepared by hand and aged for 4-6 weeks, allowing it to develop its distinct flavor and texture. This careful process has been passed down through generations and is accredited with PDO status.

4) Description of Smell and Taste:
Upon opening the wax coating, you will be greeted with a delightful aroma of beer and cheese. The creamy texture of the cheese is perfectly balanced with the distinct flavor of Theakstons Old Peculier ale. The combination of locally sourced milk and traditional methods create a unique and delicious taste that is sure to please any cheese lover.

5) Serving Suggestions:
For the best experience, we recommend serving Old Peculier Swaledale Cheese at room temperature. It can be enjoyed on its own or paired with crusty bread, sliced pear, and a pink peppercorn and honey dressing. This cheese also works well in a classic Yorkshire rarebit. For the perfect pairing, we suggest a glass of Theakstons Old Peculier ale.

6) Quality Assurance:
Don't just take our word for it, Old Peculier Swaledale Cheese has received numerous awards and accolades, including PDO status. It is recognized worldwide for its exceptional quality and taste. As the Reed family continues to follow traditional methods and use locally sourced ingredients, you can trust that each bite of this cheese is of the highest quality. As one satisfied customer put it, "This cheese is a true taste of Yorkshire."
