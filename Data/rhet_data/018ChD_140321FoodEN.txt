

1) Description of Cheese: Swaledale Goats Cheese, handcrafted by the Reed family in Richmond, Yorkshire Dales. This cheese has a rich history, dating back to the 11th century when Cistercian monks first brought cheese making techniques to the area.

2) Description of Product: This hard pressed yet moist goats milk cheese is made using locally sourced milk and vegetarian rennet. It is shaped into a round wheel, weighing approximately 1.8kg, and coated with a thin layer of wax for preservation.

3) Description of Process: The Swaledale Goats cheese is gently smoked over oak chippings for 2 days, resulting in a creamy, moist cheese with a mild smoked flavor and a rich velvety texture. It is then aged for 4-6 weeks, allowing the flavors to develop and mature.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: The gentle smoking process gives this cheese a unique and enticing aroma, with hints of oak and a subtle smokiness. The taste profile is creamy and mild, with a delicate smoked flavor that lingers on the palate.

5) Serving Suggestions: For the best experience, serve this cheese at room temperature to allow the flavors to fully develop. It can be presented on a cheese board with crackers, fruits, and nuts, or melted over a warm baguette. Pair with a crisp white wine or a full-bodied red for a perfect balance of flavors.

6) Quality Assurance: Swaledale Goats cheese holds the prestigious Protected Designation of Origin (PDO) status, ensuring that it is made according to traditional methods and using locally sourced ingredients. It has also received numerous awards and accolades, solidifying its reputation as one of the finest cheeses from the Yorkshire Dales. As one satisfied customer put it, "This cheese is a true treasure of the dales."
