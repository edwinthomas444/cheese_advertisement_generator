

1) Description of Cheese:
Introducing Swaledale Blue Cheese, a handmade, farmhouse cheese made from locally sourced cows milk by the Reed family in Richmond, Yorkshire. Its origins date back to the 11th century when Cistercian monks brought cheese making techniques from Normandy to the Yorkshire dales.

2) Description of Product:
This award-winning cheese is hard pressed yet moist, with a creamy color and distinctive blue veins running throughout. The cheese is coated in a blue wax and comes in a 250g size (approx). It is made using vegetarian rennet, ensuring it is suitable for all dietary preferences.

3) Description of Process:
Swaledale Blue Cheese is prepared using traditional methods passed down from the monks, and is aged for 4-6 weeks to achieve its perfect texture and flavor. The Reed family's recipe and methods are protected with PDO status, ensuring the highest quality and authenticity.

4) Description of Smell and Taste:
Upon opening, you'll be greeted with a delightful aroma and a soft, moist open texture. The cheese has a smooth nutty flavor with a slight tang, making it a perfect balance of flavors. No wonder it came second in the world cheese awards 2011!

5) Serving Suggestions:
For the ultimate cheese experience, serve Swaledale Blue Cheese at room temperature to allow the flavors to fully develop. It pairs perfectly with a beefy red wine, or can be used to make a decadent blue cheese sauce. Its beautiful presentation also makes it a great addition to any cheese board.

6) Quality Assurance:
Don't just take our word for it, Swaledale Blue Cheese has received numerous accolades and awards, including the prestigious PDO status. Its quality and taste are recognized worldwide, ensuring the future production and quality of this cherished treasure from the dales.
