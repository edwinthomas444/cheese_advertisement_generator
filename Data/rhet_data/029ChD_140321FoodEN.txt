

1) Description of Cheese: White Stilton, produced by six licensed dairies in Derbyshire, Nottinghamshire, and Leicestershire, is a Protected Designation of Origin cheese with a rich history and geographical significance.

2) Description of Product: White Stilton is made from locally produced milk that is pasteurized before use. It is only produced in a cylindrical shape and is never pressed. The cheese has a crumbly, open texture and a fresh, creamy background flavor. It is best enjoyed when young, at 3 or 4 weeks of age. The cheese is available in various sizes and weights.

3) Description of Process: White Stilton is made using traditional methods and is aged to perfection. The milk is pasteurized and then blended with other ingredients to create a unique, crumbly texture. The cheese is then aged for 3 or 4 weeks, allowing its flavors to develop.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: White Stilton has a distinct aroma and a rich, creamy taste. Its crumbly texture adds to its unique flavor profile. The cheese has a mild tanginess and a subtle sweetness, making it a versatile addition to any dish.

5) Serving Suggestions: White Stilton is best served at room temperature to bring out its full flavor. It can be presented on a cheese platter or used in various dishes, both savory and sweet. Pair it with fresh fruits, crackers, or bread for a delicious snack. For a more indulgent experience, try it with a glass of white wine.

6) Quality Assurance: White Stilton has received numerous awards and accolades for its exceptional quality and taste. According to cheese experts, it is a must-try for any cheese lover. Its production process follows strict guidelines, ensuring the highest quality cheese for consumers to enjoy.
