

1) Description of Cheese:
Caerphilly is a traditional cheese originally produced in the South Wales town of the same name. Today, it is mainly made by specialist crumbly cheese makers in Lancashire, Cheshire, and Shropshire. However, there is still one producer in South Wales, and South Caernarfon Creameries in North Wales also produces Caerphilly. This cheese has a rich history and geographical provenance that makes it a unique and sought-after product.

2) Description of Product:
Caerphilly cheese has a distinctive shape and size, usually in block form but sometimes in shallow wheels for supermarket pre-packs. It has a crumbly texture and a tough coat, making it easy to eat with dirty hands. The cheese is made from cow's milk and uses rennet as a coagulant. Its more mature variant is aged for up to a year, resulting in a harder texture and stronger taste.

3) Description of Process:
The process of making Caerphilly cheese involves careful preparation and aging. The cheese is made using traditional methods, and the aging process is what gives it its unique characteristics. The young cheese is a firm favorite among mining communities, while the more mature variant develops its own tough coat and becomes stronger in taste with age.

Caerphilly cheese has a pleasant tang and a clean taste. Its aroma is subtle yet distinct, making it a favorite among cheese lovers. The more mature variant has a stronger smell and taste, making it a perfect choice for those who enjoy a bold and robust flavor.
