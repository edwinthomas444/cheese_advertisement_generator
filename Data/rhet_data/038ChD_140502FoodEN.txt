

1) Description of Cheese:
Creamy Lancashire from Dewlay Cheesemakers in Lancashire, England. This cheese has a rich history dating back to the 13th century and is made using a traditional recipe standardized by Joseph Gornall in the 1890s.

2) Description of Product:
Creamy Lancashire has a fluffy, open texture with a smooth, buttery finish. It is made with pasteurized cow's milk and rennet, and comes in various sizes and weights.

3) Description of the Process:
Creamy Lancashire is made by blending curd from multiple days of milk, a unique process specific to Lancashire cheese. It is then aged for 4-12 weeks to achieve its creamy texture and rich flavor.

4) Description of Smell and Taste:
This cheese has a distinct aroma of fresh milk and a rich, buttery flavor with a hint of nuttiness. The longer it is aged, the more pronounced the nutty flavor becomes.

5) Serving Suggestions:
Creamy Lancashire is best enjoyed at room temperature, either on its own or as a topping for dishes like Welsh Rarebit or cheese on toast. It also pairs well with fruit cakes, apples, and salads. For a complete experience, pair it with a glass of real ale or a cheeseboard.

6) Quality Assurance:
Creamy Lancashire has received numerous awards and accolades, including being named the best cheese on toast in the world. It is also regularly tested for quality and consistency, ensuring the highest standards for every batch produced. As one of the nine small cheesemakers in the Preston area, Dewlay Cheesemakers takes pride in producing top-quality Lancashire cheese.
