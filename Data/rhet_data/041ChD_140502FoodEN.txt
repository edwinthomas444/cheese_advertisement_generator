

1) Description of Cheese:
Blue Stilton Cheese, made by the renowned cheesemakers Colston Bassett Dairy, Cropwell Bishop Creamery, Long Clawson Dairy, Tuxford & Tebbutt and Websters Dairy, has a rich history and geographical provenance. It takes its name from a village just south of Peterborough and was a coaching stop on the Great North Road. Its fame spread up and down the road, with The Bell Inn in the village selling large amounts of Stilton. Today, it is honored with a certification trademark and Protected Designation of Origin status, meaning it can only be made in the counties of Nottinghamshire, Derbyshire, and Leicestershire.

2) Description of Product:
Blue Stilton is a cylindrical-shaped cheese with a weight of around 250 grams. It has a smooth and creamy texture, with a slightly acidic flavor when young. As it matures, the texture becomes softer and creamier with a mellow flavor. It is coated with its own crust and has magical blue veins radiating from the center. The cheese is made from pasteurized milk from the three counties and is never pressed. The rennet used is a closely guarded secret, passed down through generations.

3) Description of Process:
The process of making Blue Stilton is a closely guarded secret, with a recipe that has been passed down through generations. The milk is pasteurized before use and the cheese is allowed to form its own crust. It is then aged for a specific amount of time, which allows the flavors to develop and the texture to become softer and creamier.

4) Description of Smell and Taste:
Blue Stilton has a distinct smell and taste, with its own unique flavor profile. It has a slightly acidic taste when young, which develops into a mellow and creamy flavor as it matures. The smell is pungent and earthy, with hints of blue mold. The combination of the smell and taste creates a unique and delicious experience for cheese lovers.

5) Serving Suggestions:
Blue Stilton is best served at room temperature, allowing the flavors to fully develop. It can be presented on a cheese board with crackers and fruits, or used in hot or cold recipes. It makes an excellent dessert cheese and is traditionally served with Port at Christmas. However, it can be enjoyed all year round and is worth trying with a dark cream sherry or a sweet dessert wine.

6) Quality Assurance:
Blue Stilton is a cheese that has stood the test of time and has been recognized for its quality and taste. It has been referred to as the "King of English Cheeses" and has been honored with a certification trademark and Protected Designation of Origin status. It has also won numerous awards and has been praised by cheese connoisseurs around the world. As the saying goes, "You can't go wrong with a good Stilton."
