

1) Description of Cheese: Ashdown Foresters, made by High Weald Dairy in the Sussex village of Horsted Keynes, takes its name from the famous Ashdown Forest in the High Weald of Sussex, known for its heathland and its inspiration for AA Milne's Winnie the Pooh.

2) Description of Product: This un-pressed cheese is firm with a slightly open texture and an attractive basket weave pattern on its surface. It comes in a medium strength and has a rounded, sharp, buttery character with a hint of saltiness and savory and lemon notes. It is available in both standard and organic versions, as well as an oak smoked variety.

3) Description of Process: It takes 8 hours to make Ashdown Forester cheese, but the true magic happens during its 3-month maturation process in carefully controlled conditions. This allows for the flavors to develop and mature to perfection.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: The Ashdown Forester cheese has a unique aroma, with hints of butter, salt, and savory notes. Its taste is well-rounded, with a sharp and buttery character, balanced with a slight saltiness and savory and lemon back notes.

5) Serving Suggestions: For the best experience, serve Ashdown Forester cheese at room temperature to allow for its full flavor to come through. It can be presented on a cheese board with crackers or bread, and pairs well with fruits and nuts. For a more indulgent experience, try it with a glass of red wine, such as a Pinot Noir or Merlot.

6) Quality Assurance: Don't just take our word for it, Ashdown Forester cheese has received rave reviews and awards for its quality and taste. It can often be found in local National Trust restaurants, as well as local markets and delis. Try it for yourself and taste the award-winning flavors of Ashdown Forester cheese.
