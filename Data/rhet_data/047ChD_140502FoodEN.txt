

1) Description of Cheese: Buxton Blue, made by the now closed Hartington Creamery, is a cousin of Blue Stilton. This cheese has a Protected Designation of Origin (PDO) and can only be made in the environs of Buxton from milk produced in Derbyshire, Nottinghamshire, and Staffordshire.

2) Description of Product: Buxton Blue is a lightly veined cheese with a deep russet color. It has a tangy flavor and is perfect with a chilled glass of sweet dessert wine. This cheese is similar in appearance and taste to Shropshire Blue. It weighs approximately 6 pounds and is made from cow's milk and vegetarian rennet.

3) Description of the Process: Buxton Blue is made using traditional methods and aged for a minimum of 8 weeks. The cheese is carefully crafted in the environs of Buxton, ensuring the highest quality and authenticity.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: Buxton Blue has a distinct aroma and a tangy, rich flavor. The light veining adds depth to the taste, making it a favorite among cheese connoisseurs.

5) Serving Suggestions: For the best experience, serve Buxton Blue at room temperature. Its deep russet color and unique flavor make it a beautiful addition to any cheese platter. It pairs well with sweet dessert wines and is also delicious with crackers or bread.

6) Quality Assurance: Buxton Blue has received numerous awards and accolades, including a PDO designation. Its quality and authenticity are guaranteed, making it a must-try for cheese lovers.
