

1) Description of Cheese: Saint Giles, made by High Weald Dairy in Sussex, is a semi soft cheese with a rich history. It was created through a "cheese naming" competition in the local area and is named after the Norman village church in Horsted Keynes.

2) Description of Product: This English cheese is similar to the continental style Saint Paulin or Port Salut, and is made with organic cows' milk from Tremains farm. It has a creamy, buttery texture and a mild flavor. The stunning edible orange rind adds a pop of color to this cheese.

3) Description of Process: Saint Giles is prepared and aged using traditional methods, resulting in a semi soft and creamy cheese. The aging process allows the flavors to develop and create a unique taste.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: The aroma of Saint Giles is rich and inviting, with hints of butter and cream. The taste is smooth and creamy, with a slight tang and a subtle sweetness. It's the perfect balance of flavors for a delicious cheese experience.

5) Serving Suggestions: For the best experience, serve Saint Giles at room temperature. Its creamy texture and mild flavor make it a versatile cheese that can be enjoyed on its own or paired with crackers and fruit. It also pairs well with a variety of wines, such as a crisp white or a light red.

6) Quality Assurance: Saint Giles has received high praise and recognition, with quotations from satisfied customers and awards for its quality. It has been tested and proven to have a fat content of 27%, protein content of 20%, and energy of 324 kcal/100g.
