

1) Description of Cheese: Abbaye de Belloc, produced by the Benedictine monks at Abbaye Notre-Dame de Belloc in the Pays Basque region of Aquitaine, France.

2) Description of Product: This traditional, farmhouse cheese is made from unpasteurized sheep's milk and comes in a flat wheel shape. It has a natural, crusty, brownish rind with patches of red, orange and yellow. The cheese weighs between four to ten months and is available in two variations - younger wheels made in December and older wheels made in July.

3) Description of Process: The Benedictine monks at Abbaye Notre-Dame de Belloc follow a cheese making process that dates back to 3000 years. They use sheep milk available in the locality and age the cheese for four to ten months. The result is a firm, dense and creamy texture with a distinctive lanolin aroma.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: Abbaye de Belloc has a unique taste that resembles burnt caramel. The cheese has a rich and creamy texture with a strong lanolin aroma. The natural rind is marked with tiny craters, adding to the unique sensory experience.

5) Serving Suggestions: For the best experience, serve Abbaye de Belloc at room temperature. When presenting the cheese, keep the natural rind intact for a rustic and authentic look. This cheese pairs well with a variety of foods such as fruits, nuts, and cured meats. For a complete experience, pair it with a bold red wine like Cabernet Sauvignon.

6) Quality Assurance: Abbaye de Belloc has received numerous accolades and awards for its exceptional quality. The cheese has been quoted by renowned cheese experts and has been subjected to rigorous quality tests to ensure consistency in taste and texture. Trust in the expertise of the Benedictine monks and indulge in the deliciousness of Abbaye de Belloc.
