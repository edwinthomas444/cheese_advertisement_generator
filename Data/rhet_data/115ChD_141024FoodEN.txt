

1) Description of Cheese: Waitrose Blue Cheese, produced by the renowned British supermarket chain Waitrose, originates from the United Kingdom. This cheese has a rich history, dating back to the 17th century, and is a beloved staple in British cuisine.

2) Description of Product: Waitrose Blue Cheese is a semi-hard, crumbly cheese with a creamy white color and distinctive blue veins running through it. It comes in a variety of shapes and sizes, ranging from small wedges to larger blocks. Made from pasteurized cow's milk and rennet, this cheese has a tangy, piquant, spicy, and peppery flavor.

3) Description of Process: Waitrose Blue Cheese is made by adding a special mold culture to the milk at the beginning of the cheese-making process. The cheese is then aged for several months, allowing the mold to develop and create the characteristic blue veins. This careful process results in a high-quality, flavorful cheese.
