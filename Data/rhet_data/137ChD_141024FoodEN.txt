

1) Description of Cheese: Lincolnshire Poacher is a premium cheese produced by the renowned manufacturer, Waitrose. It is named after the traditional English folk song and is made in the county of Lincolnshire, England.

2) Description of Product: This robust full-flavoured hard cheese is made from unpasteurised milk from Holstein cows. It has a pale yellow color and a grainy brown rind that is inedible. The cheese has a similar earthy flavor to Cheddar but has a more open texture. It weighs approximately 2.5kg and is available in a chunk shape.

3) Description of Process: The cheese is carefully prepared using traditional methods and is aged for a minimum of 14 months. During this time, it develops its unique flavor and texture, making it a truly exceptional cheese.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: The Lincolnshire Poacher has a distinct aroma that is both earthy and nutty. Its full-bodied flavor is rich and creamy, with a subtle tanginess that lingers on the palate.

5) Serving Suggestions: To fully enjoy the flavors of this cheese, it is best served at room temperature. It can be presented on a cheeseboard and paired with pickled onions and crusty bread for a quick and delicious lunch. For a more formal setting, it can be grated or grilled and used in a variety of dishes. It pairs perfectly with a crisp white wine or a full-bodied red.

6) Quality Assurance: This award-winning cheese has received numerous accolades, including a gold medal at the British Cheese Awards in 2001. It is also available from the service counter at the prestigious Daventry and Newark branches of Waitrose. Customers rave about its exceptional quality and flavor, making it a must-try for all cheese lovers.
