

1) Description of Cheese: Cropwell Bishop Shropshire Blue is a premium cheese made in the village of Cropwell Bishop in Nottinghamshire, England. It is produced at a small family-run creamery, ensuring the highest quality and traditional methods are used.

2) Description of Product: This cheese has a distinctive shape and size, with a creamy, crumbly texture. It is coated in a beautiful blue mold, giving it a unique appearance. Made from cow's milk and using traditional rennet, this cheese has a rich and creamy taste.

3) Description of Process: Cropwell Bishop Shropshire Blue is carefully prepared and aged to perfection. The cheese is made using traditional methods, with a focus on quality and attention to detail. It is then aged to develop its full flavor and texture.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: The aroma of this cheese is rich and creamy, with a hint of spiciness. The taste is well-balanced, with a medium level of spiciness that adds depth to the creamy texture. It has a unique and satisfying flavor that is sure to please any cheese lover.

5) Serving Suggestions: For the best experience, serve Cropwell Bishop Shropshire Blue at room temperature. It makes a stunning addition to any cheeseboard, especially when paired with other English cheeses. It also adds a delicious touch to baked potatoes or cheese sauces. For the perfect pairing, enjoy with a glass of red wine.

6) Quality Assurance: Don't just take our word for it, Cropwell Bishop Shropshire Blue has won two gold medals at the British Cheese Awards in 2001 for Best Blue and Best Traditional British Cheese. It is also suitable for vegetarians and is available at Waitrose. Trust in the quality and tradition of this award-winning cheese.
