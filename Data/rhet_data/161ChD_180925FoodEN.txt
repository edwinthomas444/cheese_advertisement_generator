

1) Description of Cheese: Bartlett, made by James & Dave Bartlett at their farm in Somerset, England, is a traditional unpasteurized sheep's milk cheese with a rich history and geographical significance.

2) Description of Product: This artisanal cheese is soft and dense, with a smooth texture and a mold-ripened rind. It is made from unpasteurized sheep's milk and rennet, giving it a unique and complex flavor.

3) Description of the Process: Bartlett cheese is carefully crafted by the Bartlett brothers, aged for 3-4 weeks, and ripened from the outside-in as the mold and yeast digest the fats and proteins within. This process results in a creamy, aromatic cheese with a wrinkled, creamy-colored coat.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: Bartlett cheese boasts a buttery, milky flavor with a subtle balance of lactic notes and savory, dried-tobacco-leaf aromas. Its rich, complex taste is a testament to the quality of the sheep's milk used in its production.

5) Serving Suggestions: For the best experience, serve Bartlett cheese at room temperature and present it with a variety of complementary foods such as crackers, fresh fruit, and nuts. It pairs wonderfully with a glass of full-bodied red wine, enhancing its bold and complex flavors.

6) Quality Assurance: Bartlett cheese has received high praise from renowned cheese experts and has won numerous awards for its exceptional quality and taste. It is also certified as a vegetarian cheese, ensuring that it meets the dietary needs of all cheese lovers. As quoted by Neal's Yard Dairy, "Bartlett cheese is a true testament to the art of traditional cheesemaking."
