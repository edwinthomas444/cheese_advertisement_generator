

1) Description of Cheese:
Berkswell cheese, produced by the Berkswell Cheese Company at Ram Hall Farm in West Midlands, United Kingdom, is a hard, smear-ripened cheese with a rich history and geographical provenance.

2) Description of Product:
This cheese is made from unpasteurized sheep's milk and has a fat content of 48%. It has a chewy, dense and firm texture, and is coated with a natural, pale yellow rind. The cheese is made in small vats and is left to mature for at least 6 months. The moulds used give the cheese its distinctive shape.

3) Description of the Process:
The cheese is made using traditional methods, with unpasteurized ewes' milk and animal rennet. The cheese is left to mature in plastic kitchen colanders, which gives it its unique shape. The cheese is then aged for at least 6 months, allowing it to develop its complex flavors.

4) Description of Smell and Taste:
Berkswell cheese has a strong, goaty aroma and a tangy, acidic flavor. The cheese has a rich and robust taste, with a hint of sweetness and a slight nuttiness.

5) Serving Suggestions:
Berkswell cheese is best served at room temperature, allowing its flavors to fully develop. It can be presented on a cheese board with crackers and fruits, or used as a topping for salads and pasta dishes. This cheese pairs well with a full-bodied red wine, such as a Cabernet Sauvignon or Merlot.

6) Quality Assurance:
Berkswell cheese has received numerous awards and accolades, including being named as an "ace producer" by the Fletcher family at Ram Hall Farm. The cheese is made with high-quality ingredients and follows strict quality control measures, ensuring a consistently delicious product. As a testament to its quality, Berkswell cheese is not only popular in the United Kingdom, but is also exported to other countries around the world.
