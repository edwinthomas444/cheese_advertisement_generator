

1) Description of Cheese: Blue Wensleydale, produced by Wensleydale Creamery in the town of Hawes, is a historic cheese with origins dating back to 1150 in Wensleydale, North Yorkshire, England. It was traditionally made by Cistercian monks and is now produced by local farmers' wives.

2) Description of Product: Blue Wensleydale is a hard, blue-veined cheese made from a combination of pasteurized cow's milk and sheep's milk for enhanced flavor. It is shaped into a round wheel, weighing approximately 5-6 pounds, and has a close texture. The cheese is coated in a cloth wrap.

3) Description of the Process: The art of making Blue Wensleydale has been passed down from the monks to the farmers' wives, who continue to handcraft the cheese. It is matured for six months and lightly pressed for 24 hours before being wrapped in muslin cloth.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: Blue Wensleydale has a rich aroma and a mellow flavor with hints of acidity, saltiness, and savory sharpness. Its strong taste appeals to both newcomers and cheese connoisseurs alike.

5) Serving Suggestions: For the best experience, serve Blue Wensleydale at room temperature to allow its flavors to fully develop. It can be presented on a cheese board or crumbled over salads and pastas. This cheese pairs well with a variety of foods, including fruits, nuts, and cured meats. For a perfect pairing, try it with a bold red wine.

6) Quality Assurance: "Blue Wensleydale - a handcrafted cheese made exclusively by Wensleydale Dairy Products" has been awarded a PDO designation to protect its name and quality. It has also received numerous accolades and is subject to strict quality tests to ensure its exceptional taste and texture.
