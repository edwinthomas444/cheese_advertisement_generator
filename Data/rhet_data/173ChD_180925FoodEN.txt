

1) Description of Cheese: Buffalo is a unique and award-winning cheese crafted by the renowned English cheese maker, Nick Hodgetts. Created in 1996, it was the first buffalo cheese in Britain and has since gained recognition for its exceptional taste and quality. Made from unpasteurized water buffalo's milk, it hails from the Worcestershire region in the West Midlands of England.

2) Description of Product: This artisanal Mozzarella cheese is a hard, wheel-shaped cheese with a creamy and firm texture. It is coated with a natural, leathery rind and has a weight of around four months. The cheese is made using vegetarian rennet, making it suitable for vegetarians.

3) Description of Process: The milk for Buffalo cheese is sourced from a farm in Idlicote, near Stratford-upon-Avon, and is then carefully prepared and aged by Mr. Hodgetts himself. The aging process takes around four months in a maturing room, allowing the cheese to develop its unique flavors and textures.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: Buffalo cheese has a delicate and subtle flavor, with hints of sweetness and nuttiness. As it ages, the cheese becomes firmer and creamier, giving off notes of almonds and lemony zest. Its aroma is fresh and aromatic, adding to its overall appeal.

5) Serving Suggestions: For the best experience, Buffalo cheese should be served at room temperature. It can be presented on a cheese board or added to various dishes, such as salads or pasta. It pairs well with a variety of foods, including fruits, nuts, and cured meats. For a complete dining experience, we recommend pairing it with a crisp white wine.

6) Quality Assurance: Buffalo cheese has been recognized for its exceptional taste and quality, winning the award for Best New or Experimental Cheese at the British Cheese Awards. It has also been quoted as Britain's first buffalo cheese, further solidifying its reputation in the cheese world. Each batch of Buffalo cheese undergoes rigorous quality tests to ensure that it meets the highest standards of taste and quality.
