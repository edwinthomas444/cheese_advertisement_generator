

1) Description of Cheese: Cheshire, produced by Appleby's, is a traditional British cheese with a rich history dating back to Roman times. It is made in the English counties of Cheshire, Denbighshire, Flintshire, Shropshire, and Staffordshire.

2) Description of Product: Cheshire cheese is a hard cheese made from pasteurized cow's milk. It has a crumbly and dense texture, with a cloth-wrapped rind. The cheese is typically orange in color and has a fat content of 48%. It is made using vegetarian rennet, making it suitable for vegetarians.

3) Description of Process: The cheese is made using traditional methods, with a focus on quality and taste. It is aged to perfection, allowing the flavor to develop and mature. The aging process also results in a firmer and darker cheese, with a full-flavored taste and no hint of bitterness.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: Cheshire cheese has a full-flavored, mild, and milky taste with a tangy aftertaste. It has a distinct aromatic smell that adds to its overall experience.

5) Serving Suggestions: For the best experience, serve Cheshire cheese at room temperature. It can be presented in a crumbled form on fruits, chutney, vegetables, and baked dishes. It pairs well with a variety of foods, including wine, making it a versatile addition to any meal.

6) Quality Assurance: Cheshire cheese has been recognized for its exceptional quality and taste. It has received numerous awards and accolades, including being mentioned in the Domesday Book of the 11th century. With its lower salt content, it is a healthier option for cheese lovers.
