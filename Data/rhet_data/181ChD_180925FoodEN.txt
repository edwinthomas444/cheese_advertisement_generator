

1) Description of Cheese:
Colston Bassett Stilton is a renowned blue cheese made by Colston Bassett Dairy Limited, located in the region of Nottinghamshire, England. This small dairy has been producing Stilton cheese for generations, using traditional methods and locally sourced ingredients.

2) Description of Product:
This artisan cheese is semi-hard and made from pasteurized cow's milk, with a fat content of 33%. It has a distinctive shape and size, with a close, creamy texture and a natural, slightly sticky orange-brown rind. The cheese is coated with patches of white mould and has a weight that varies depending on the age.

3) Description of Process:
Colston Bassett Stilton is made using hand-ladled curd and animal rennet, a method that sets it apart from other Stilton makers. The cheese is then aged for several months, allowing for a more luscious, creamy and full-bodied texture, and a complex and deep flavour.

4) Description of Smell and Taste:
The smell of Colston Bassett Stilton is earthy and lactic, with a hint of blue mould and butter. Its flavour is a perfect balance of these elements, resulting in a buttery, fruity, and tangy taste that will delight any cheese connoisseur. As the cheese ages, the tanginess fades, giving way to a more mellow and savory flavour.

5) Serving Suggestions:
To fully appreciate the flavours and textures of Colston Bassett Stilton, it is best served at room temperature. The cheese can be presented on a cheese board, accompanied by crackers, fruits, and nuts. It pairs well with a variety of foods, including pears, apples, and honey. For a more indulgent experience, try it with a glass of port or a full-bodied red wine.

6) Quality Assurance:
Colston Bassett Stilton has received numerous accolades, including a gold medal at the 2014 World Cheese Awards. Its quality is also attested by renowned cheese experts, such as Neal's Yard Dairy. As a result, you can be assured that every bite of Colston Bassett Stilton is of the highest quality and will leave you wanting more.
