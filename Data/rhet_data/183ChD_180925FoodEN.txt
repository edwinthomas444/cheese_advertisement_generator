

1) Description of Cheese: Cote Hill Blue is a unique and award-winning cheese produced by Michael and Mary Davenport at their Cote Hill Farm in Lincolnshire, England. Made from unpasteurized cow's milk, this cheese has a rich history and geographical provenance that adds to its exceptional quality and taste.

2) Description of Product: Cote Hill Blue is a soft, artisan, blue-veined cheese with a creamy texture and natural rind. It is made from the milk of Friesian cows, which are milked twice a day and the morning's milk is used to make this cheese. The cheese is injected with Penicillium roqueforti and rubbed in salt, giving it a salty sharpness, buttery sweetness, and a subtle kick. It weighs approximately 250g and is best enjoyed within 10 days of opening.

3) Description of Process: Cote Hill Blue is carefully prepared and aged to perfection. The raw milk used in the production of this cheese is rich in butterfat and protein, giving it a unique and delicious flavor. The cheese is aged for a specific period, allowing it to develop its distinctive blue-veined texture and complex flavors.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: Cote Hill Blue has a strong and smoky aroma, with hints of butter and salt. The taste is a perfect balance of buttery, salty, sharp, and smoky flavors, making it a delight for cheese lovers. The creamy texture adds to the overall experience, making it a must-try for all cheese connoisseurs.

5) Serving Suggestions: For the best experience, Cote Hill Blue should be served at room temperature. It can be presented on a cheese board, accompanied by crackers, fruits, and nuts. This cheese pairs well with baked potatoes, lasagne, or can be enjoyed as a cheese snack. For wine pairing, we recommend a full-bodied red wine such as a Cabernet Sauvignon or a rich and creamy Chardonnay.

6) Quality Assurance: Cote Hill Blue has received numerous awards and recognition for its exceptional quality and taste. It won a gold medal at the British Cheese Awards in its first year of production and a Super Gold award at the 2014 World Cheese Awards. It has also been declared as one of the world's 62 best cheeses. As a testament to its quality, Cote Hill Blue is loved and recommended by cheese experts and enthusiasts alike.
