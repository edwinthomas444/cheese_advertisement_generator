
1) Description of Cheese:
Cotherstone cheese, created by Joan and Alwin Cross in Teesdale, is a semi-hard, artisan cheese that originated in the village and parish of Cotherstone in County Durham, England. It is related to other famous British cheeses such as Wensleydale and Swaledale.

2) Description of Product:
Cotherstone cheese is made from unpasteurized cow's milk, specifically full-fat Jersey cow's milk. It is a semi-hard cheese with a crumbly and open texture, coated with wax and weighing approximately 1kg. It is available in two versions - a white version and a blue-veined version, also known as "Yorkshire Stilton".

3) Description of Process:
The cheese is handmade using traditional methods and is aged for a minimum of 3 months. It belongs to the "Dale style" of cheeses, which are known for their soft and crumbly texture, lemony and tangy taste, and are typically consumed when young.

4) Description of Smell and Taste:
Cotherstone cheese has a fresh aroma and a tangy, citrusy taste with a slight acidity. The blue-veined version has a more pronounced taste and a distinctive blue-veined appearance.

5) Serving Suggestions:
For the best taste and texture, Cotherstone cheese should be served at room temperature. It can be presented on a cheese platter or used in tarts and pastry dishes. It pairs well with a crisp Sauvignon Blanc or a fruity red wine.

6) Quality Assurance:
Cotherstone cheese is suitable for vegetarians and has received numerous accolades and awards, including being named "Yorkshire Stilton". It is also regularly tested for quality and freshness to ensure the best taste and experience for consumers.
