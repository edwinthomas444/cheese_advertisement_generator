

1) Description of Cheese: Criffel, made by Loch Arthur Creamery in Dumfriesshire, Scotland, is a cheese with a rich historical and geographical provenance. This Scottish cheese is named after the famous Criffel hill and is a testament to the traditional cheese-making techniques of the region.

2) Description of Product: Criffel is a semi-hard, artisan cheese made from unpasteurized cow's milk. The cheese has a creamy and smooth texture, and its straw-colored rind is washed to perfection. This cheese is available in various sizes and weights, making it a versatile choice for any occasion. The only ingredients used in making Criffel are high-quality cow's milk and rennet.

3) Description of Process: Criffel is made using traditional methods, with a focus on preserving the authentic taste and texture of Scottish cheese. The cheese is prepared by hand and aged to perfection, giving it a unique flavor and aroma. The aging process allows the cheese to develop a pink-orange rind, which adds to its visual appeal.

The cheese has a smooth and creamy flavor that is sure to delight any cheese lover. Its unique taste and aroma make it a must-try for anyone looking to experience the true essence of Scottish cheese.
