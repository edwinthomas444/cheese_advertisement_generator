

1) Description of Cheese:
Waterloo cheese, produced by Village Maid Cheese, is a semi-soft, artisan cheese made from unpasteurized cow's milk. Its origins can be traced back to the picturesque village of Riseley in Berkshire, England. This cheese is a testament to the rich history and tradition of British cheese-making.

2) Description of Product:
Waterloo cheese is a round-shaped cheese with a creamy, firm and smooth texture. It is coated with a washed rind, giving it a characteristic yellow color. With a weight of 45%, this full-fat cheese is made using only the finest unpasteurized Guernsey milk and rennet sourced from a local farm near Henley.

3) Description of Process:
The cheese-making process of Waterloo cheese involves the use of the traditional washed curd method. This method helps to balance the acidity and results in a soft, buttery flavor. The cheese is then aged for a period of 4 to 10 weeks, allowing it to develop its unique and distinct taste.

4) Description of Smell and Taste:
Waterloo cheese has a delightful earthy aroma, reminiscent of the lush green pastures where the cows graze. Its taste is a perfect balance of rich, creamy sweetness and subtle herb and grass undertones. The creamy, buttery texture melts in your mouth, leaving a lingering, satisfying aftertaste.

5) Serving Suggestions:
To fully experience the flavors and textures of Waterloo cheese, it is best served at room temperature. It can be presented on a cheese board, sliced or cubed, and paired with a variety of crackers, bread, and fruits. For a perfect food pairing, try it with some honey or fig jam. For a wine pairing, opt for a light and fruity white wine such as Sauvignon Blanc.

6) Quality Assurance:
Renowned food writer, Sarah Freedman, describes Waterloo cheese as "luscious and creamy with the sweetness of rich milk and undertones of herbs and grass." It has also won numerous awards, including the prestigious British Cheese Awards. Rest assured, this cheese has undergone rigorous quality tests to ensure its exceptional taste and quality.
