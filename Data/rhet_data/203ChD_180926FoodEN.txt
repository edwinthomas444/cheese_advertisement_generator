

1) Wellesley by Hill Farm Dairy and Neal's Yard Dairy is a cheese made from unpasteurized goat's milk in Stawley, England. It is named after the Duke of Wellington and has a rich history and geographical provenance.

2) This artisan cheese has a hard texture and is slightly crumbly yet firm. It is light cream to white in color and is coated with a washed rind. It weighs approximately 6 months and is made with high-quality ingredients such as unpasteurized goat's milk and rennet.

3) The process of making Wellesley involves salting the cheese and then maturing it for up to six months. During this time, the cheese is regularly turned to develop its complex and delicious flavors.

4) Wellesley has a nutty and sweet flavor with hints of grass and straw. Its aroma is also grassy, making it a unique and flavorful cheese.

5) To fully enjoy the flavors of Wellesley, we recommend serving it at room temperature and presenting it on a cheese board with crackers, fruits, and nuts.
