

1) Description of Cheese: Double Gloucester is a traditional, full fat, hard cheese made from pasteurized or unpasteurized cow's milk. It is produced by Taw Valley Creamery in Gloucestershire, England and has a rich history dating back to 1498 when it was first made in the City of Gloucester.

2) Description of Product: This artisan cheese is known for its unique characteristics, including a crumbly, dense and firm texture. It has a golden yellow color and is coated with a natural rind. Double Gloucester is made from the milk of once nearly extinct Old Gloucester cows and uses vegetarian rennet. It comes in both single and double varieties, with the double variety being twice the height and more flavorful.

3) Description of Process: Double Gloucester is made using the whole milk taken from two milking or a mixture of milk and cream. After being aged for at least 4 months, the cheese develops a smooth and buttery texture, rich and nutty yet mellow flavor, and a beautiful apricot color thanks to the addition of annatto plant extract.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: The aroma of Double Gloucester is sweet and inviting, while the flavor is creamy, mellow, and nutty. As the cheese ages, it develops more complex and nutty flavors, and the texture becomes harder and flaky.

5) Serving Suggestions: For the best experience, Double Gloucester should be served at room temperature and presented on a cheese board with crackers, fruits, and nuts. It pairs perfectly with traditional British Ale or a red wine like Syrah. It is also a great addition to sandwiches, salads, and pasta dishes.

6) Quality Assurance: Double Gloucester has received numerous international awards, including a Silver medal at the 2014 World Cheese Awards. It is also a participant in the Cooper's Hill Cheese-Rolling and Wake, where its quality and sustainability are evaluated by buyers. With its rich history, unique characteristics, and numerous accolades, Double Gloucester is a high-quality cheese that is sure to satisfy any cheese lover's palate.
