

1) Description of Cheese: Double Worcester by Anstey's of Worcester, England - a smaller and tastier variation of Double Gloucester, made from pasteurized cow's milk and originating from the historic city of Worcester in England, Great Britain and United Kingdom.

2) Description of Product: Double Worcester is a hard, artisan cheese with a cylinder shape and a natural rind. It weighs around 5-7 months of affinage and has a firm, flaky texture and a straw yellow interior. It is made with pasteurized cow's milk, vegetarian rennet, and traditionally colored with annatto.

3) Description of Process: Double Worcester is carefully crafted by Colin and Alyson Anstey on their Broomhill Farm in Worcester. The cheese undergoes a meticulous preparation and aging process, resulting in its unique texture and flavor.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: Double Worcester has a distinct aroma of citrus and nuttiness. Its flavor is a delightful combination of sharpness and tanginess, making it a truly indulgent experience for cheese lovers.

5) Serving Suggestions: For the best taste, serve Double Worcester at room temperature. It can be presented on a cheese board with crackers, fruits, and nuts. It pairs well with a variety of foods, including sandwiches, salads, and pasta dishes. For a perfect wine pairing, try it with a crisp white wine or a fruity red.

6) Quality Assurance: Double Worcester has been recognized for its exceptional quality, winning a silver medal at the British Cheese Awards in 2001. It has also undergone rigorous quality tests to ensure its taste and safety. As quoted by satisfied customers, "Double Worcester is a must-try for any cheese connoisseur!"
