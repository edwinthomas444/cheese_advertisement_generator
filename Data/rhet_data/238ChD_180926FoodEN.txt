

1) Description of Cheese: Dunsyre Blue by H.J. Errington & Co is a traditional, artisan cheese made from unpasteurized cow's milk in Carnwath, Lanarkshire, Scotland. Its name comes from the small village near Lanark in the Clyde Valley, where it has been made for the past 25 years.

2) Description of Product: This soft, blue-veined cheese has a creamy, crumbly and smooth texture. It is coated with mold and has a cream-colored interior. Dunsyre Blue is made using unpasteurized milk from Ayrshire cows and rennet, giving it a unique and authentic taste.

3) Description of Process: Dunsyre Blue is a handmade cheese, carefully crafted and aged to perfection. The cheese is ripened with mold, which gives it its distinct blue-green veins. This process results in a complex and rich flavor that is truly one-of-a-kind.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: The strong aroma of Dunsyre Blue is a testament to its quality and aging process. The cheese has a salty, sharp and spicy flavor, with a slightly sweet undertone. Each bite leaves a long-lasting aftertaste, making it a truly memorable experience.

5) Serving Suggestions: To fully appreciate the flavors of Dunsyre Blue, it is best served at room temperature. The cheese can be presented on a cheese board, paired with crackers, fruits, and nuts. For a perfect combination, enjoy it with a glass of whisky or a Rhone wine.

6) Quality Assurance: Dunsyre Blue has received numerous awards and accolades, including being named the best cheese in Scotland. It has also been praised by renowned cheese experts for its exceptional taste and quality.
