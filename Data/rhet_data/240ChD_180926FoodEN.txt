

1) Description of Cheese: Extra Mature Wensleydale from Wensleydale Creamery in North Yorkshire, United Kingdom. This historic cheese has been made since 1150 by Cistercian monks and is now produced mainly from pasteurized cow's milk with added sheep's milk for enhanced flavor.

2) Description of Product: Extra Mature Wensleydale is a hard, artisan cheese with a crumbly, dense, and grainy texture. It is cloth-wrapped and has a yellow color. Made from pasteurized cow's milk with a fat content of 48%, this cheese is matured for nine months to develop its strong and sharp flavor.

3) Description of Process: The art of making Wensleydale cheese was passed down from the monks to the farmer's wives, who continue to produce it at their farmhouses. The cheese is aged in an atmospherically-controlled room for nine months at the Wensleydale Creamery in Hawes. This process results in a full-flavored and robust cheese.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: Extra Mature Wensleydale has a strong aroma and a full-flavored taste with notes of milkiness, sharpness, and strength. Its rich and robust flavors make it the king of cheeseboards.

5) Serving Suggestions: Serve Extra Mature Wensleydale at room temperature for optimal flavor. It pairs well with fruit cake, apples, and hot desserts like fruit pies and crumbles. It is also a perfect accompaniment during lunch or tea time. For a complete experience, pair it with a fruity white wine such as Pinot Grigio.

6) Quality Assurance: Wensleydale Creamery is seeking to protect the name Yorkshire Wensleydale Cheese under PDO designation, ensuring its authenticity and quality. It has also received numerous awards and accolades for its exceptional taste and quality.
