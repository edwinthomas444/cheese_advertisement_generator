

1) Description of Cheese: Lairobell, produced by Hattie and Jock Bell of Scotland at their East Lairo, Shapinsay farm on Orkney Isles, is a true representation of the rich history and geographical diversity of Scotland and the United Kingdom.

2) Description of Product: This artisan cheese is made from unpasteurized goat's milk, giving it a unique and distinct flavor. It is sold in cylindrical shapes and weighs between two to eight weeks of curing and maturation, resulting in a creamy, crumbly, and open texture. The natural rind adds to its authenticity and the pale yellow color is a testament to its high-quality ingredients.

3) Description of the Process: Lairobell is carefully crafted using traditional methods, ensuring the cheese is of the highest quality. The cheese is made only from spring to autumn, when the goats are at their peak, resulting in a rich and flavorful cheese. The two to eight weeks of curing and maturation process allows for deep flavors to develop and the correct consistency to be achieved.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: Lairobell has a herbaceous and sweet flavor with undertones of almonds and herbs. Its aroma is distinctively goaty, adding to the unique experience of this cheese.

5) Serving Suggestions: For the best experience, serve Lairobell at room temperature to allow the flavors to fully develop. This cheese is perfect for salads, soups, and as a creamy addition to any dish. Pair it with a crisp white wine or a full-bodied red for a perfect balance of flavors.

6) Quality Assurance: Lairobell has received numerous awards and accolades, including being a favorite among cheese connoisseurs. Its high-quality ingredients and traditional production methods are evident in every bite. As Hattie and Jock Bell say, "Lairobell is not just a cheese, it's a labor of love."
