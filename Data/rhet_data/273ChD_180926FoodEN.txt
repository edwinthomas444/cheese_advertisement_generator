

1) Description of Cheese:
Marbled Cheddar is a unique and delicious cheese produced by blending white and orange cheddars. This particular cheese is made by the renowned manufacturer, England and United Kingdom, known for their high-quality dairy products. It has a rich history and is produced in the beautiful countryside of England.

2) Description of Product:
Marbled Cheddar is a semi-soft cheese that comes in a variety of textures. It can be creamy or firm, depending on the batch. Its shape and size vary, but it typically comes in a block form. The cheese has a smooth coating and weighs approximately 200 grams. It is made from pasteurized cow's milk and uses rennet as a coagulant.

3) Description of Process:
The process of making Marbled Cheddar involves blending white and orange cheddars, resulting in a beautiful golden orange color. The cheese is then aged to perfection, allowing for a smooth and buttery texture. This process gives the cheese its unique and delicious taste.

4) Description of Smell and Taste:
Marbled Cheddar has a mild and smooth flavor with a hint of sweetness and tanginess. Its aroma is inviting and adds to the overall experience of enjoying this cheese. The combination of Colby and Monterey Jack or white and orange Cheddar curds gives it a complex and satisfying taste.

5) Serving Suggestions:
For the best experience, we recommend serving Marbled Cheddar at room temperature. It can be presented on a cheese platter with fruits, nuts, and crackers, or melted over dishes such as quiches, omelets, or vegetables. This cheese pairs well with a variety of wines, especially reds like Merlot or Cabernet Sauvignon.
