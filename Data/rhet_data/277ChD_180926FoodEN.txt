

1) Description of Cheese: Northumberland Cheese Company's award-winning Coquetdale is a farmhouse cheese from Northumberland, England. Founded in 1984 by Mark Robertson, the company produces a range of 16 handmade cheeses using locally sourced milk from cows, sheep, goats, and jersey.

2) Description of Product: Coquetdale is a semi-hard cheese with a natural rind and a creamy texture. It is made from cow's milk, rennet, and salt. Each wheel of Coquetdale weighs approximately 2kg and has a distinctive cylindrical shape.

3) Description of Process: Coquetdale is prepared using traditional methods and aged for 4-6 months to develop its unique flavor. The cheese is carefully turned and brushed with salt to enhance its taste and texture.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: Coquetdale has a rich, nutty aroma with hints of grass and herbs. Its flavor is complex, with a balance of savory and tangy notes. The cheese has a smooth and creamy texture that melts in your mouth.

5) Serving Suggestions: Coquetdale is best served at room temperature to bring out its full flavor. It can be presented on a cheese board with crackers, fruit, and nuts. This versatile cheese pairs well with a variety of foods, including cured meats, pickles, and chutneys. For a perfect pairing, try it with a full-bodied red wine like Cabernet Sauvignon.

6) Quality Assurance: Coquetdale has won numerous awards, including the Gold Medal at the British Cheese Awards. It has also received high ratings from quality tests, with critics praising its unique flavor and texture. According to one reviewer, "Coquetdale is a true representation of the quality and craftsmanship of Northumberland Cheese Company."
