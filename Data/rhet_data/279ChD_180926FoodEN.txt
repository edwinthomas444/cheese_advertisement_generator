

1) Description of Cheese: Ogleshield, made by Village Stores in Cadbury, Somerset, is a semi-soft, artisan cheese with a rich history. It is made from unpasteurized cow's milk and hails from England, Great Britain and the United Kingdom.

2) Description of Product: Ogleshield is a creamy and supple cheese with a washed rind and a cream color. It is made using beautifully rich raw Jersey milk by Jamie Montgomery & Wayne Mitchell of the renowned Montgomery cheese making family. The cheese is also available in alternative spellings such as Ogle Shield and Ogleshield Raclette.

3) Description of Process: To achieve its unique flavor, Ogleshield is washed in special brine every three days. This process softens the cheese paste and intensifies its fruity and sweet flavor. It is a cross between Montgomery's Cheddar and a Tomme, making it perfect for cooking as it melts brilliantly.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: The aroma of Ogleshield is pungent, adding a depth of flavor to the cheese. Its taste is a perfect balance of fruity and sweet notes, making it a crowd favorite.

5) Serving Suggestions: For the best experience, serve Ogleshield at room temperature. Its creamy texture and fruity flavor make it a great addition to a cheese board or as a topping for a warm dish. It pairs well with a variety of foods, such as cured meats, fruits, and nuts. For a complete dining experience, pair it with a glass of red wine.

6) Quality Assurance: Ogleshield has been recognized for its exceptional quality, winning a silver award at the 2014 World Cheese Awards. It also undergoes rigorous quality tests to ensure that every bite is as delicious as the last. As quoted by its producers, Village Stores, "Ogleshield is a cheese that is made with passion and dedication, resulting in a truly exceptional product."
