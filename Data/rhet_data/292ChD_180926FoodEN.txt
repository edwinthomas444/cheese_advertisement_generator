

1) Description of Cheese: Perroche is a delicious, artisan cheese made from unpasteurized goat's milk. It is produced by Neal's Yard Dairy in England and is named after Beatrice Garroche, one of the original creators of this cheese in the early 1980s.

2) Description of Product: This fresh, soft cheese has a fluffy texture and is available in a variety of sizes and coatings. It is made using traditional animal rennet, giving it a unique and authentic flavor.

3) Description of Process: Perroche is carefully crafted by Charlie Westhead and Haydn Roberts at Neal Yard's Creamery in Herefordshire, West Midlands. The cheese is aged to perfection, resulting in a creamy and structured texture that sets it apart from other fresh cheeses.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: The aroma of Perroche is aromatic and fresh, with hints of goatiness and herbs. The flavor is a perfect balance of tangy, salty, and savory notes, with a bright, lemony finish that lingers on the palate.

5) Serving Suggestions: For the best experience, serve Perroche at room temperature to fully appreciate its flavors and textures. It can be presented plain or rolled in tarragon, dill, or rosemary for an added burst of flavor. This cheese pairs perfectly with crusty bread, crackers, and fresh fruit. For a complete culinary experience, pair it with a crisp white wine or a fruity red.

6) Quality Assurance: Perroche has received high praise from cheese experts and consumers alike. It has been awarded numerous accolades, including the prestigious World Cheese Award. With its traditional production methods and high-quality ingredients, you can trust that every bite of Perroche is a true taste sensation.
