

1) Description of Cheese:
Sharpham Elmhirst is a luxurious triple cream cheese manufactured by Sharpham Wine & Cheese in Devon, England. Its name is derived from entrepreneur Ruth Ash's maiden name, who played a key role in establishing cheese production at Sharpham in the early 1980s.

2) Description of Product:
This soft-ripened cheese is made from unpasteurized Jersey cow's milk and has a bloomy rind that covers a light and silky textured white paste. It is available in a variety of sizes and weights, and its creamy, smooth texture makes it perfect for spreading. The cheese is coated with a white rind and is suitable for vegetarians.

3) Description of Process:
Sharpham Elmhirst is a mould-ripened cheese, inspired by the French cheese 'Vignotte'. It undergoes a careful process of preparation and aging, resulting in a delicate flavor when young, which develops into a rich and creamy taste as it matures. It is one of only two triple cream cheeses produced in the UK.

4) Description of Smell and Taste:
The cheese has a fresh, mild aroma with hints of milk and richness. Its flavor is creamy, full-bodied, and smooth, making it a delight for cheese lovers. As it ages, the taste becomes more pronounced, offering a luxurious and indulgent experience.

5) Serving Suggestions:
For the best experience, Sharpham Elmhirst should be served at room temperature, allowing its flavors to fully develop. It can be presented on a cheese board with crackers, fruits, and nuts, or used as a spread on bread or crackers. This cheese pairs well with a variety of wines, such as Chardonnay or Pinot Noir.

6) Quality Assurance:
Sharpham Elmhirst has received numerous awards, including a gold medal at the Taste of the West Awards and a bronze medal at the British Cheese Awards. Its quality and taste have been praised by cheese enthusiasts and experts alike, making it a must-try for any cheese connoisseur.
