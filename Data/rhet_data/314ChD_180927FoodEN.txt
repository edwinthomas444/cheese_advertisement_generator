

1) Description of Cheese:
Introducing the award-winning Appleby's Cheshire cheese, made by the Appleby family since 1952. This traditional cloth-bound unpasteurised Cheshire cheese hails from the British Isles and is a true testament to the rich history of cheese-making in this region.

2) Description of Product:
Appleby's Cheshire cheese is a hard cheese with a unique, crumbly texture and a zesty, full-bodied and tangy flavour. It is made using raw cows' milk, salt, animal rennet, and annatto, giving it a beautiful pale orange color. Each piece is hand-crafted and weighs between six weeks to six months, ensuring the highest quality and taste.

3) Description of the Process:
At Hawkstone Abbey Farm in Shropshire, the Appleby family follows a traditional recipe passed down for three generations. The cheese is made using unpasteurised milk from their own herd of cows, and the land's unique mineral qualities add a distinctive saline flavor. The cheese is then aged for up to six months, allowing the flavors to develop and mature.

4) Description of Smell and Taste:
Appleby's Cheshire cheese has a complex aroma that will tantalize your senses. Its tangy and zesty flavor is complemented by a subtle hint of saltiness, creating a perfect balance of flavors. The texture is crumbly yet creamy, making it a delight to taste.

5) Serving Suggestions:
For the best experience, we recommend serving Appleby's Cheshire cheese at room temperature. It can be presented on a cheese board or paired with your favorite crackers and fruits. This versatile cheese also pairs well with a variety of wines, from crisp whites to full-bodied reds.

6) Quality Assurance:
Don't just take our word for it, Appleby's Cheshire cheese has won numerous awards and accolades, including being recorded in the Doomsday Book of 1086. Our commitment to quality is reflected in every piece of cheese we cut and wrap by hand, ensuring the freshest taste for our customers.
