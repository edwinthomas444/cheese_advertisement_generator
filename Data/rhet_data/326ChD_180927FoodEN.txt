

1) Description of Cheese: Sparkenhoe Red Leicester is a unique and delicious cheese made by David and Jo Clarke on Sparkenhoe Farm in the British Isles. This unpasteurised cheese has a rich history, being the first Red Leicester produced on the farm since 1875.

2) Description of Product: Sparkenhoe Red Leicester is a hard cheese with a creamy and mellow flavour. It is made from raw cows' milk, with the addition of salt, rennet, starter culture, and natural annatto for its deep orange-red colour. Each piece is freshly cut and wrapped to order, ensuring the highest quality and freshness. 

3) Description of the Process: David and Jo Clarke use traditional methods to make Sparkenhoe Red Leicester, starting with the milk from their top herd of pedigree Holstein-Friesian cattle. The cheese is then aged to perfection, resulting in a slightly flakey texture and a unique taste. 

4) Description of Smell and Taste: Sparkenhoe Red Leicester has a distinct and inviting aroma, with hints of cream and earthiness. Its flavour is creamy and mellow, with a slight tang and a nutty finish. The addition of natural annatto gives the cheese a beautiful deep orange-red colour, making it a feast for the senses. 

5) Serving Suggestions: For the best experience, we recommend serving Sparkenhoe Red Leicester at room temperature, allowing its full flavour to develop. It can be presented on a cheese board as a centerpiece, or grated over dishes for a burst of flavour. This versatile cheese pairs well with a variety of foods, including crackers, fruit, and cured meats. For a truly indulgent experience, pair it with a glass of full-bodied red wine. 

6) Quality Assurance: At Sparkenhoe, we take pride in sourcing, caring for, and selling the best cheese. Our commitment to quality is evident in every piece, which is freshly cut and wrapped by hand. We are proud to say that our cheese has received numerous awards and accolades, and is loved by cheese connoisseurs around the world.
