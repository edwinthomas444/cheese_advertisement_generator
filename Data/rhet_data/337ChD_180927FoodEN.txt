

1) Description of Cheese: Wigmore is a delicate and creamy ewes’ milk cheese made in Berkshire by the Wigmores. Its heavenly texture and delicacy make it a cheese of real refinement. The cheese is of British Isles origin and has a rich history, with its roots dating back to 1984 when Anne Wigmore began making cheese at the National Institute for Research in Dairying.

2) Description of Product: Wigmore is a soft cheese with a weight of either 400g or 800g, or a 200g cut. It has a gentle flavour and is coated in a smooth texture. The cheese is made from ewes’ milk, starter culture, rennet, and salt. It is also suitable for vegetarians.

3) Description of Process: Wigmore cheese is made using the washed curd method, where an amount of whey is replaced with water during the make. This results in a gentle, delicate flavour and a smooth texture. The cheese is then aged to perfection, ensuring a complex and fruity taste.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: Wigmore cheese has a complex and fruity aroma, with a rich and unctuous taste. It has a great delicacy of taste and texture, making it a true delight for the senses.

5) Serving Suggestions: For the best experience, serve Wigmore cheese at a temperature below 8˚C. It can be presented as a whole cheese in 400g or 800g weights, or as a 200g cut. This cheese pairs well with fruits, crackers, and bread, and is perfect for a cheese board. For a complete indulgence, pair it with a glass of white wine.

6) Quality Assurance: Wigmore cheese has been recognized for its excellence, winning the gold award at the 2017 Artisan Cheese Awards. It is also freshly cut and wrapped to order, ensuring the highest quality and freshness. As quoted by the cheese maker, Anne Wigmore, "I have a great feel for the cheese and its technicalities." Wigmore cheese is also made with the utmost care and precision, making it a truly exceptional cheese.
