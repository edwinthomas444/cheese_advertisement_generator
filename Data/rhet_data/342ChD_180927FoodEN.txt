

1) Description of Cheese: Lord of the Hundreds is a delicious unpasteurised English ewes' cheese, made by the Dyball family at the Traditional Cheese Dairy in Stonegate, East Sussex. This cheese has a rich historical and geographical provenance, with its name originating from Saxon times and its production taking place in the British Isles.

2) Description of Product: Lord of the Hundreds is a hard cheese with a grainy texture, aged for depth of flavour and coated with a delicate, rich milk that leaves a creamy, sweet taste in the mouth. It is made from raw ewes' milk, salt, starter culture, and microbial rennet. The cheese is freshly cut and wrapped to order, with a weight of 100g per serving.

3) Description of Process: Lord of the Hundreds is made using traditional methods, with a commitment to producing high quality, hand-made cheeses. The raw milk is sourced from single farm herds and the cheese is aged to perfection, giving it a real depth of flavour.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: Lord of the Hundreds has a strong, delicious aroma that is reminiscent of an Italian Pecorino. The taste is rich and creamy, with a depth of flavour that is balanced by a sweet, delicate milkiness.

5) Serving Suggestions: For the best experience, we recommend serving Lord of the Hundreds at room temperature, to allow the flavours to fully develop. The cheese can be presented on a cheese board, accompanied by crackers and fruit. It pairs well with a variety of foods, including cured meats, figs, and honey. For a complete experience, we suggest pairing it with a full-bodied red wine.

6) Quality Assurance: Lord of the Hundreds has received numerous accolades, including a silver award at the 2017 Mondial du Fromage. The Dyball family is committed to animal welfare and has received a Good Dairy Commendation from Compassion in World Farming. This cheese is made with the highest quality ingredients and is freshly cut and wrapped to order, ensuring the best possible taste and experience for our customers. As one satisfied customer says, "This cheese is simply divine."
