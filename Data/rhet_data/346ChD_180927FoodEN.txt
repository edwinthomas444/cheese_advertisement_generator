

1) Description of Cheese:
Timsbury Ash Pyramid is a delectable cheese crafted by Mary Holbrook, a renowned cheese maker from Timsbury, located just south of Bath in the British Isles. With a rich historical and geographical provenance, this cheese is a true representation of the quality and craftsmanship of English cheese making.

2) Description of Product:
This individual cheese is made from unpasteurised goats' milk, giving it a fresh and lemony flavour. Its soft and creamy texture resembles a mousse, making it a delight to savour. The pyramid shape and light coating add to its elegant appearance, while the weight of the cheese is just right for easy handling. The rennet used in the production of this cheese is of the highest quality, ensuring a superior taste.

3) Description of the Process:
Timsbury Ash Pyramid is carefully prepared using traditional methods, ensuring that every bite is bursting with flavour. The cheese is then aged to perfection, giving it a unique and distinct taste. This process is what sets this cheese apart from others, making it a must-try for all cheese lovers.

4) Description of Smell and Taste:
The aroma of Timsbury Ash Pyramid is fresh and clean, with a hint of lemon. The taste is equally delightful, with a zesty and tangy flavour that lingers on the palate. The mousse-like texture adds to the overall experience, making this cheese a true sensory delight.

5) Serving Suggestions:
For the best taste experience, serve Timsbury Ash Pyramid at room temperature. Its elegant pyramid shape makes it a perfect addition to any cheese platter, and it pairs beautifully with fresh fruits and crackers. For a truly indulgent experience, pair this cheese with a crisp white wine, such as a Sauvignon Blanc.

6) Quality Assurance:
Timsbury Ash Pyramid has received numerous accolades, including quotations from top chefs and awards for its exceptional taste. It has also undergone rigorous quality tests, ensuring that every bite is of the highest quality. Don't miss out on this out-of-season cheese, and try Dorstone as an alternative while the goats are resting before kidding. Trust us, you won't be disappointed.
