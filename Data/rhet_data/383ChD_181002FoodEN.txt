

1) Description of Cheese: Leeds Blue by Mario Olianas, a rich and creamy cheese with a piquant flavor. Made in Adel, near Leeds, West Yorkshire by Mario Olianas, who comes from a family of Italian gastronomes.

2) Description of Product: Leeds Blue is a smooth and creamy cheese made from sheep's milk sourced from Harrogate. It has a rich texture and is coated with a delicate rind. The cheese weighs approximately 200g and is made using traditional rennet.

3) Description of Process: Mario Olianas uses a small vat and follows an adapted Gorgonzola recipe to make Leeds Blue. The cheese is aged in a specially converted room within his house, allowing it to develop its unique flavor and texture.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: Leeds Blue has a distinct piquant aroma, with hints of earthy and nutty notes. The taste is rich and creamy, with a slight tanginess that lingers on the palate.

5) Serving Suggestions: For the best experience, serve Leeds Blue at room temperature to allow the flavors to fully develop. It can be presented on a cheese board with crackers and fruits, or used in cooking to add a creamy and tangy flavor to dishes. Pair it with a full-bodied red wine for a perfect combination.

6) Quality Assurance: "A better Italian cheese than his brothers'" - The Guardian. Mario Olianas' passion and dedication to creating high-quality cheese have been recognized with numerous awards, including the Great Taste Award. Leeds Blue undergoes strict quality tests to ensure it meets the highest standards of taste and quality.
