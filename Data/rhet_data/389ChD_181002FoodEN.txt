

1) Description of Cheese:
Introducing Dorstone Goats, one of Britain's most outstanding goats' milk cheeses. Made by Charlie Westhead in Dorstone, Herefordshire, this cheese is a testament to the skill and expertise of one of the UK's finest cheese makers. Named after the local Neolithic settlement at Dorstone Hill, this cheese has a rich historical and geographical provenance.

2) Description of Product:
Dorstone Goats is a delicate, fresh, and light cheese with a mousse-like texture and a lemony-fresh flavor. It has just a hint of goat, making it a perfect choice for those who enjoy a subtle, yet distinctive taste. This cheese is sold at only three weeks old, ensuring it remains at its peak freshness. It is coated with a dark grey rind, achieved by lightly ashing the cheese. Each cheese weighs 180g and has a shelf life of two weeks.

3) Description of Process:
To create this exceptional cheese, Charlie Westhead collects goats milk from Gloucestershire daily and follows a classic French recipe. The curd is pre-drained, giving it a light, fluffy texture, and then it is aged for three weeks. The aging process involves drying the cheese and lightly ashing it, resulting in a unique and delicious flavor.

4) Description of Smell and Taste:
Dorstone Goats has a delicate aroma, with hints of lemon and a subtle goatiness. The taste is fresh and light, with a tangy and slightly nutty flavor. The mousse-like texture melts in your mouth, leaving a pleasant and lingering aftertaste.

5) Serving Suggestions:
For the best experience, serve Dorstone Goats at room temperature to allow the flavors to fully develop. This cheese is perfect for a cheese board, paired with crackers, fruits, and nuts. It also makes a great addition to salads, omelets, and sandwiches. For a perfect wine pairing, try it with a crisp white wine such as Sauvignon Blanc or a light and fruity red wine like Pinot Noir.

6) Quality Assurance:
Dorstone Goats has received numerous accolades, including being sold at The Courtyard Dairy, known for its commitment to sourcing the finest and highest quality cheeses. Charlie Westhead's eco-friendly creamery, powered by solar panels, wind turbines, and wood burners, ensures that each cheese is made with care and attention to detail. As Charlie Westhead himself says, "Our cheese is made with love and care, and I believe it tastes better for it."
