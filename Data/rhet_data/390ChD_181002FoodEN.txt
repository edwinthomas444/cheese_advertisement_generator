

1) Description of Cheese:
Introducing Tunworth Camembert, a rich and intense cheese handcrafted by Stacey Hedges and Charlotte Spruce in Herriard, Hampshire, England. This English Camembert has been perfected to make even a Frenchman weep, with its unique vegetal and porcini flavors. Its name comes from the nearby hamlet of Tunworth, near Basingstoke, giving it a strong historical and geographical provenance.

2) Description of Product:
Tunworth Camembert is a 250g cheese that is rich and creamy in texture, with a wrinkled coating that adds to its intense flavor. Made from high-quality cow's milk and traditional rennet, this cheese is a testament to the dedication and expertise of its creators.

3) Description of Process:
The process of making Tunworth Camembert is a labor of love. Stacey and Charlotte use traditional Camembert techniques, including slow acidification and hand-ladling, to create a unique and flavorful cheese. The use of undulating moulds and yeasts also adds to its distinct character. The cheese is then aged to perfection, resulting in a creamy and indulgent taste.

4) Description of Smell and Taste:
The aroma of Tunworth Camembert is rich and vegetal, with hints of porcini that will tantalize your senses. Its taste is intense and indulgent, with a perfect balance of creamy and tangy flavors. The unique combination of vegetal and porcini notes makes this cheese a true delight for the taste buds.

5) Serving Suggestions:
To fully appreciate the flavors of Tunworth Camembert, it is best served at room temperature. The cheese can be presented on a cheese board, accompanied by crackers, fruits, and nuts. For the perfect food pairing, try it with crusty bread and a drizzle of honey. For a complete experience, pair it with a glass of full-bodied red wine.

6) Quality Assurance:
Don't just take our word for it, Tunworth Camembert has been awarded the prestigious Supreme Champion Cheese in 2013 at the British Cheese Awards. Stacey's dedication to creating the perfect Camembert has also been recognized by winning the award twice, in 2006 and 2013.
