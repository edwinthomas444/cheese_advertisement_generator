

1) Description of Cheese: Dale End Cheddar from Botton Creamery in Yorkshire is a strong and tangy cheddar with a rich and long-lasting flavor. It is made by a Camphill Community that employs adults with learning disabilities, making it not only a delicious cheese but also a socially responsible choice. 

2) Description of Product: This traditional clothbound cheddar is made from unpasteurized milk from 46 Dairy Shorthorn cows, known for their high-quality milk. Each individual mini-truckle weighs between 900g-1kg and is wrapped in a cloth coating. The cheese is made using traditional methods and aged for up to 18 months, resulting in a well-developed and full-flavored cheese. 

3) Description of Process: Alastair Pearson, a local lad who learned his cheese-making trade in Germany, carefully selects specific batches of cheese for The Courtyard Dairy. The cheese is then aged to perfection, allowing it to develop its distinctive tangy bite and rich flavor. 

4) Description of Smell and Taste: Dale End Cheddar has a strong and tangy aroma, with hints of earthiness and grassiness. The flavor is rich and complex, with a sharp cheddar bite that lingers on the palate. 

5) Serving Suggestions: For the best experience, serve Dale End Cheddar at room temperature to allow the flavors to fully develop. It can be presented on a cheese board with crackers and fruits, or melted into a delicious grilled cheese sandwich. This cheese pairs well with full-bodied red wines, such as Cabernet Sauvignon, and is also perfect for cooking, adding depth and flavor to any dish. 

6) Quality Assurance: This cheese has been featured on Paul Hollywood's Pies & Puds Programme, showcasing its exceptional taste and quality. It has also received rave reviews from customers and has won multiple awards for its flavor and production process. With a shelf life of two weeks, you can be assured that you are receiving a fresh and high-quality product.
