

1) Description of Cheese:
Moorland Tomme is a strong and savoury cheese made on Botton Camphill Farming Community for adults with learning disabilities. This traditional Tomme-style cheese is made by Alastair Pearson at Botton Creamery in North Yorkshire, England.

2) Description of Product:
This cheese is made using rich Dairy Shorthorn milk and aged for 1 year, resulting in a strong and powerful flavor. It is made in a round shape and weighs approximately 1 kilogram. The cheese has a firm texture and is coated in a natural rind. It is made using traditional methods and rennet.

3) Description of Process:
Alastair Pearson, a local lad who learned his cheese-making trade in Germany, only makes a small amount of this Tomme. The milk used comes from Botton Camphill Farming Community's 46 Dairy Shorthorn cows, known for producing high-quality milk. The cheese is aged for 1 year, allowing it to develop its rich and savory flavor.

4) Description of Smell and Taste:
Moorland Tomme has a nutty and slightly sweet flavor, with a sharp Cheddar bite. Its aroma is strong and powerful, reflecting the quality of the milk used and the traditional methods of production.

5) Serving Suggestions:
For the best experience, serve this cheese at room temperature. It can be presented on a cheese board, accompanied by crackers, bread, and fruits. This cheese pairs well with a full-bodied red wine, such as a Cabernet Sauvignon or Merlot. It also complements savory dishes, such as charcuterie boards or grilled meats.

6) Quality Assurance:
Alastair Pearson's cheese has received recognition for its quality and flavor. Andy Swinscoe, owner of The Courtyard Dairy, discovered this cheese and decided to stock it in his store. The cheese has also won awards for its taste and production process. The Botton Camphill Farming Community is fully self-sustainable and biodynamic, ensuring the highest quality of ingredients and production methods.
