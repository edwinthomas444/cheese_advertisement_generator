

1) Description of Cheese: Corra Linn by Errington family, made in Carnwath, Scotland. This hard sheep's milk cheese is reminiscent of the finest Manchego, capturing the rugged Scottish landscape.

2) Description of Product: Corra Linn is a unique wheel of extra-matured cheese, aged for 2 years, giving it a more intense and rich flavor. It is made with unpasteurized sheep's milk, using the Lacaune breed (used for Roquefort cheese), and is coated with a cloth held on by local rapeseed oil.

3) Description of Process: The cheese is made by Selina Errington, daughter of the founder, using traditional methods. The milk is sourced from their own flock of 450 sheep, and the cheese is cloth-bound and aged for 9 months at The Courtyard Dairy.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: Corra Linn has a sweet hazelnutty flavor with earthy, heathery undertones. Its aroma is reminiscent of the rugged Scottish landscape, making it a truly unique and flavorful cheese.

5) Serving Suggestions: For the best experience, serve Corra Linn at room temperature. Its unique shape and size make it a great addition to any cheese platter. Pair it with crackers, fruits, and nuts for a delicious snack, or use it in cooking for added depth of flavor. For a perfect pairing, enjoy Corra Linn with a glass of red wine.

6) Quality Assurance: "The fabulous rich milk provided by the Lacaune breed of sheep proved perfect for a hard sheep's milk cheese made to a Manchego recipe." - Andy Swinscoe, owner of The Courtyard Dairy. Corra Linn has also won numerous awards for its exceptional flavor and quality. Rest assured, this cheese has been thoroughly tested and approved by cheese experts.
