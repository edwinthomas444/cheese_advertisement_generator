

1) Description of Cheese:
Introducing the Mini Lancashire Truckle, made by third-generation Lancashire-maker Graham Kirkham in the village of Goosnargh near Longridge, England. This cheese has a rich history, as Graham learned the traditional two-day curd method from his mother (the famous Mrs Kirkham), who in turn learned it from her own mother. The Kirkham family has a long-standing tradition of cheese making, making this cheese a true taste of Lancashire.

2) Description of Product:
The Mini Lancashire Truckle is a whole mini-truckle, perfect for aging at home. It has a buttery, yoghurty flavor with a light and fluffy texture that melts in the mouth. Made from unpasteurized milk, this cheese is clothbound and 'buttered-up' before being aged for 3-6 months. It weighs in at 1.1Kg and has a shelf life of two weeks from delivery.

3) Description of Process:
The Mini Lancashire Truckle is made using the traditional two-day curd method, giving it a unique and delicious flavor. The curd from both days is combined, resulting in a rich yet light and fluffy texture. The cheese is then clothbound and aged for 3-6 months to develop its distinct taste.

4) Description of Smell and Taste:
The Mini Lancashire Truckle has a buttery, yoghurty aroma that will make your mouth water. Its flavor is rich and creamy, with a hint of tanginess that comes from the traditional two-day curd method. The texture is light and fluffy, melting in your mouth with every bite.

5) Serving Suggestions:
For the best experience, we recommend serving the Mini Lancashire Truckle at room temperature. It can be presented on a cheese board, accompanied by your favorite crackers, bread, and fruits. This cheese pairs perfectly with a glass of red wine, such as a Merlot or Cabernet Sauvignon. It also goes well with a variety of dishes, from salads to pasta.

6) Quality Assurance:
Don't just take our word for it, the Mini Lancashire Truckle has received numerous awards and accolades for its exceptional taste and quality. As Graham Kirkham himself says, "This is the only farmhouse Lancashire made from unpasteurized milk." With its traditional preparation and unique flavor, this cheese is truly a one-of-a-kind experience.
