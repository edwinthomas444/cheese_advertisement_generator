

1) Description of Cheese - Richard III Wensleydale by Fortmayne Dairy, from Richmond, North Yorkshire, England. This artisan-produced cheese is made using an ancient recipe and is named after the childhood home of Richard III.

2) Description of Product - This cheese is sold at one month old, giving it a zesty and fresh flavor. It has a clean and light taste with gentle, milky, and lemony notes. It is sold in the traditional manner and weighs approximately 100g per person. The cheese is made using sheep's milk and rennet.

3) Description of the Process - This cheese is made to a traditional pre-war recipe, passed down from Suzanne Stirke's grandmother. It is aged for one month, allowing it to develop a moister and less-crumbly texture. The production of this cheese dates back to the 17th-century and has been perfected over the years.

4) Description of Smell and Taste - Richard III Wensleydale has a clean and light aroma with hints of milk and lemon. Its flavor is delicate and refreshing, with a slight acidity that adds depth to the cheese.

5) Serving Suggestions - To fully enjoy the flavors of this cheese, it is best served at room temperature. It can be presented on a cheese board with crackers and fruits, or used in recipes such as grilled cheese sandwiches or salads. For a perfect pairing, try it with a crisp white wine.

6) Quality Assurance - This cheese has received high praise and recognition, including quotations from renowned cheese experts and awards for its exceptional quality. It has also undergone rigorous quality tests to ensure that every bite is a delicious and satisfying experience.
