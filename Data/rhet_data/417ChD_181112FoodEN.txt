

1) Description of Cheese: Darling Blue is a British blue cheese made on Doddington farm, bordering Scotland, by Maggie Maxwell in Wooler, Northumberland, England. The farm has a rich history, with the Maxwell family moving there in the 1950s and now producing a range of high-quality dairy products.

2) Description of Product: This cheese is firm-textured with delicate blue veining and a natural mottled rind. It comes in a range of sizes and weights, and is made using fresh milk from the farm's herd of Ayrshire, Friesian, and Normandy cows. The rennet used is of the highest quality, ensuring a delicious and authentic flavor.

3) Description of Process: Darling Blue is carefully crafted by Maggie, who has years of experience in cheese-making. The cheese is matured for 3 months, allowing the flavors to develop and the texture to become smooth and creamy. The natural rind is formed during this process, adding to the unique character of the cheese.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: The aroma of Darling Blue is rich and inviting, with hints of biscuity flavors. On the palate, the cheese is smooth and gentle, with a complex and rich flavor. The blue veining adds a subtle tanginess, making it a perfect balance of flavors.

5) Serving Suggestions: For the best experience, serve Darling Blue at room temperature to allow the flavors to fully develop. It can be presented on a cheese board with crackers and fruit, or used as a delicious addition to salads and sandwiches. For a perfect pairing, try it with a crisp white wine or a rich red.

6) Quality Assurance: Darling Blue has received high praise from cheese experts and consumers alike. It has won numerous awards for its exceptional taste and quality, and is regularly tested to ensure it meets the highest standards. As Maggie says, "Our cheese is made with love and care, and we are proud to share it with the world."
