

1) Description of Cheese: Cheshire Cheese, made by the Appleby family of Hawkstone Abbey Farm, is a beloved cheese in England with a rich history. It is believed to be the oldest cheese made in Britain, dating back to the Roman era. The Romans even built a walled city, Chester, to control the area where this precious cheese was made.

2) Description of Product: Cheshire Cheese is a rustic, dry cheese with a slightly crumbly texture and a pale orange color. It is made from raw cow's milk from Friesian cattle in the Cheshire area. The cheese is formed into cloth-wrapped drums, weighing about 15 lbs, and aged for 6 months to a year. It is important to seek out Genuine Farmhouse Cheshire, which is stamped with the farm number, production date, and quality grading.

3) Description of Process: Cheshire Cheese is made using traditional methods and aged to perfection. The cheese is formed into drums and wrapped in cloth, then aged for 6 months to a year. This allows the cheese to develop its distinctive flavor, with a slightly salty and buttery taste.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: Cheshire Cheese has a unique aroma, thanks to the underlying layer of bedrock salt in the Cheshire basin area. Its flavor is quintessentially cheesy, with a mild and slightly salty taste. The texture is drier than Cheddar, but still crumbly like Cheddar.

5) Serving Suggestions: To fully enjoy the flavor of Cheshire Cheese, it is best served at room temperature. It can be presented on a cheese board with crackers and fruits, or used in cooking for a delicious twist on classic dishes. For a perfect pairing, try Cheshire Cheese with radishes, watercress, or celery when in season. It also pairs well with a variety of wines, such as Chardonnay or Pinot Noir.

6) Quality Assurance: Cheshire Cheese has been praised by cheese connoisseurs and has received numerous awards for its exceptional quality. According to The Complete Book of Cheese by Robert Carlton Brown, Cheshire is "not only the most literary cheese in England, but the oldest." The Royal Navy even stocks its ships with this beloved cheese. Look for the Genuine Farmhouse Cheshire, produced by the Appleby family, for the best quality and taste.
