

1) Description of Cheese: Westcombe Cheddar is a fantastic example of a West Country farmhouse cheddar, made by Tom Calver of Westcombe Dairy in the Batcombe Vale of Somerset, England. This renowned cheese has been made since 1890 and is known for its high quality and traditional methods.

2) Description of Product: This cheddar cheese has a deep, complex flavor with a mellow lactic tang and long notes of citrus, hazelnut, and caramel. It has a structured and firm texture, with a smooth breakdown that lingers on your palate. Each cut cheese is approximately 12 days old and weighs approximately 100g. The ingredients include cows' milk, salt, rennet, and dairy cultures.

3) Description of Process: Westcombe Cheddar is made using traditional methods, with unpasteurized milk and pint starters. It is also made using traditional rennet, making it a Westcountry Farmhouse Cheddar which is Protected Designation of Origin (PDO). The cheese is aged in special conditions in the cheese aging room, allowing the milk to express itself and enhance the flavor.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: This cheddar cheese has a slightly earthy smell and a creamy, rich taste. The flavors are complex and well-balanced, with a mellow tang and notes of citrus, hazelnut, and caramel. The texture is smooth and firm, with a lingering aftertaste that leaves you wanting more.

5) Serving Suggestions: For the best taste experience, it is recommended to serve Westcombe Cheddar at room temperature. This cheese pairs perfectly with a crisp, refreshing cider. It can be presented on a cheese board or used in cooking to add depth and flavor to dishes. For a delicious pairing, try it with crackers, fruit, and nuts.

6) Quality Assurance: Westcombe Cheddar has received numerous awards, including Best Cheddar and Best PDO/PGI Cheese at the British Cheese Awards. It is also made with the highest quality ingredients and traditional methods, ensuring a consistently delicious and authentic cheese. As quoted by Tom Calver, "From the richness of the milk to the special conditions of our cheese aging room, everything we do revolves around letting the milk express itself in the finished cheese." Trust in the quality and taste of Westcombe Cheddar.
