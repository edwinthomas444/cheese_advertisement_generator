

1) Description of Cheese:
Introducing Cheshire Appleby, a traditional cloth bound Cheshire cheese that has been produced by the Appleby family for generations. Made in the Shropshire region of England, this cheese is known for its unique crumbly texture and rich, clean flavor. The Appleby family is the only remaining producer of this authentic cheese, which is made with milk from their own farm.

2) Description of Product:
Cheshire Appleby is a cow's milk cheese that is unpasteurized and made with only the finest ingredients: milk, starter culture, rennet, salt, and annatto. It is shaped into a round wheel and coated with cloth after pressing. Each wheel weighs approximately [insert weight] and is known for its moist and crumbly texture.

3) Description of Process:
The process of making Cheshire Appleby involves scalding the curds at a lower temperature than cheddar, which helps retain moisture and create a crumbly texture. After cutting, draining, milling, and salting, the curds are left overnight before being pressed. This allows for natural drainage and creates an open texture. The cheese is then aged on the farm, under the expert guidance of cheesemaker Garry Gray.

4) Description of Smell and Taste:
Cheshire Appleby has a clean, zesty aroma that is followed by a rich and mouth-watering finish. The flavor is both bold and delicate, with a perfect balance of acidity and sweetness. The taste lingers on the tongue, making it a truly unforgettable cheese.

5) Serving Suggestions:
For the best experience, serve Cheshire Appleby at room temperature. To enhance its flavor, pair it with fruit cake, figs, or dates.
