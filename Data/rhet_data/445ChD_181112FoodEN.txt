

1) Description of Cheese: Baron Bigod by Fen Farm, Suffolk, England
Baron Bigod is a traditional raw milk, farmhouse brie made by Jonny and Dulcie Crickmore at Fen Farm in Bungay, Suffolk. It is one of the few remaining farmhouse bries in the world and the only one in the UK. With its rich history and geographical provenance, Baron Bigod is a true gem among cheeses.

2) Description of Product: Silky smooth texture, earthy and mushroom flavors, 250g weight
Baron Bigod is known for its silky smooth texture and delicious earthy and mushroom flavors. When ripe, the rind has a slight brownish marbling, which is a desirable feature as it indicates a gooey and spreadable interior. This cheese is made from the raw milk of Fen Farm's own Montbeliarde cows, giving it a unique and rich taste. It weighs 250g, making it the perfect size for sharing or indulging on your own.

3) Description of Process: Traditional preparation and aging
Baron Bigod is made using traditional methods, ensuring the highest quality and authentic taste. The milk from the Montbeliarde cows is prized among cheesemakers around the world for its perfect cheesemaking properties. The cheese is then aged to perfection, allowing the flavors to develop and intensify.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: Earthy, mushroom, and creamy
The aroma of Baron Bigod is earthy and mushroom-like, with a hint of creaminess. The taste is rich and creamy, with a subtle tanginess that lingers on the palate. The combination of flavors makes this cheese a true delight for the senses.

5) Serving Suggestions: Best served at room temperature, pairs well with crackers and chilli jam
For the best experience, serve Baron Bigod at room temperature. This allows the cheese to reach its full potential, with its flavors and textures at their peak. It pairs perfectly with Millers Elements Fire crackers for a spicy kick, or Millers Damsels Buttermilk for a milder option. For a delicious lunch, try it in a toastie with chorizo and a nice chilli jam. And for a royal touch, enjoy it just like Harry and Meghan did at their wedding breakfast.

6) Quality Assurance: "Brie of choice" at our café and shop, highly acclaimed
Baron Bigod is our brie of choice at our café and shop, and for good reason. It has received high praise and recognition, including being part of Harry and Meghan's wedding breakfast. It is also not recommended to eat during pregnancy, further emphasizing its exceptional quality. With its rich history, traditional preparation, and delicious taste, Baron Bigod is a must-try for all cheese lovers.
