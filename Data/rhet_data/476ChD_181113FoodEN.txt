

1) Description of Cheese:
Introducing Redesdale, a sweet and subtle sheep's cheese from Soppit Farm in Otterburn, England. This cheese has a rich history, dating back thirty years as the first cheese ever made by Marc Robertson's company, Redesdale Sheep's Dairy.

2) Description of Product:
Redesdale is a small batch cheese with a velvety texture and refreshing tang. It is made from pasteurized sheep's milk and is suitable for vegetarians and pregnant women. The cheese is available in various sizes, with the image shown for illustrative purposes only.

3) Description of Process:
Legend has it that Marc Robertson made the first batch of Redesdale in buckets in his kitchen sink. The cheese is made using traditional methods and is aged to perfection, resulting in a trusted and award-winning product.

4) Description of Smell and Taste:
Don't let the sheep's milk deter you - Redesdale has a clean and subtle flavor that will surprise you. Its enticing aroma and characteristic taste make it a wonderful alternative to goat's cheese.

5) Serving Suggestions:
For the best experience, we recommend serving Redesdale at room temperature. Its unique shape and size make for an impressive presentation on any cheese platter. Pair it with crackers or crusty bread, and for a perfect combination, try it with a glass of crisp white wine.

6) Quality Assurance:
Redesdale has won its fair share of awards, solidifying its place in our cheese portfolio. It is a trusted and high-quality product that has been praised by cheese lovers and experts alike. As Marc Robertson himself says, "Redesdale is extra special to us because it was the first cheese we ever made."
