

1) Description of Cheese:
Berkswell is a magnificent cheese made from unpasteurised ewe's milk by Stephen and Sheila Fletcher on their farm in Ramhall, West Midlands, England. This award-winning cheese has a rich history and is known for its superb quality and taste.

2) Description of Product:
Berkswell is a hard-rinded cheese with a distinctive amonite shape and unusual markings on its thick rind. It weighs around 3kg and has a firm texture, making it perfect for grating. The cheese is made using natural rennet and is aged for around 4 months, resulting in a delicious, mouth-filling flavor.

3) Description of Process:
Berkswell is handmade on the farm to exacting high standards. The cheese is aged for 4 months, allowing the flavors to develop and the natural rennet to be released. This traditional process ensures the cheese maintains its quality and unique taste.

4) Description of Smell and Taste:
Berkswell has a delicious lingering flavor with hints of caramel and a definite tang on the finish. The cheese has a strong, distinctive smell that is characteristic of unpasteurized ewe's milk. Its firm texture also adds to the overall taste experience.

5) Serving Suggestions:
For the best taste experience, Berkswell should be served at room temperature. Its magnificent shape and distinctive ridges make it a beautiful addition to any cheese board. It pairs well with a variety of foods, such as pasta and gratin recipes. For a perfect pairing, serve with a good Manchego and a glass of red wine.

6) Quality Assurance:
Berkswell is a regular award winner, showcasing its exceptional quality and taste. Handmade on the farm to exacting high standards, this cheese is a testament to the dedication and expertise of the Fletcher family. Its firm texture and delicious flavor have earned it a reputation as one of the best cheeses in England.
