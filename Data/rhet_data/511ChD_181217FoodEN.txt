

1) Description of Cheese: Cornish Yarg, produced by the Mead family on their farm in Cornwall, England, is a beloved cheese with a rich history. It was first made in the early 1980s by Alan Gray, who sold the recipe to farmer Michael Horrell. In 1995, the Mead family joined the production, and in 2006, all production moved to their farm, Pengreep.

2) Description of Product: Cornish Yarg is a crumbly cheese with a light texture and fresh citrus notes. It is wrapped in nettle leaves, giving it a distinctive finish and subtle herbaceous flavor. The cheese weighs approximately 8 weeks or more and is made from cow's milk using pasteurization and vegetarian rennet.

3) Description of Process: The production of Cornish Yarg begins on the Mead family farm, where the cheese is carefully crafted and aged for 8 weeks or more. The cheese is then wrapped in nettle leaves and aged further to develop its unique flavor.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: Cornish Yarg has a delightful aroma, with hints of fresh citrus and herbs. Its taste is mild to medium strength, with a crumbly texture and a subtle herbaceous flavor from the nettle leaves.

5) Serving Suggestions: To fully enjoy the flavors of Cornish Yarg, we recommend serving it at room temperature. For a stunning presentation, place the cheese on a wooden board and garnish with fresh herbs. Cornish Yarg pairs well with crisp white wines, such as Sauvignon Blanc, and is perfect for a cheese platter or as a topping for salads and sandwiches.

6) Quality Assurance: Cornish Yarg has received numerous accolades, including the prestigious World Cheese Awards. It has also been featured in top food magazines and is enjoyed by cheese lovers worldwide. Our commitment to quality and tradition is reflected in every bite of Cornish Yarg. As the Mead family says, "It's not just cheese, it's Cornish Yarg."
