

1) Description of Cheese: Cote Hill Blue is a delicious unpasteurised blue cheese made in Lincolnshire, England. It is hand-made on the farm by MICHAEL & MARY DAVENPORT and their sons Joe & Ross, using the milk of their 80 pedigree Friesian cows.

2) Description of Product: This superb cheese has a soft texture, similar to a blue brie, and is encased in a thin grey rind. It has a creamy taste with a definite sharpness and rich, complex flavors. The cheese is available in a variety of sizes and weights, ranging from 4-8 weeks old.

3) Description of Process: Cote Hill Blue is carefully prepared and aged in the heart of the Lincolnshire Wolds. The Davenport family uses traditional methods and techniques to create this exceptional cheese, ensuring the highest quality and flavor.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: Upon opening, Cote Hill Blue has a distinct and enticing aroma that will awaken your senses. The taste is creamy, with a perfect balance of sharpness and complex flavors that will leave you wanting more.

5) Serving Suggestions: For the best experience, serve Cote Hill Blue at room temperature. It can be enjoyed on its own or paired with a variety of foods, such as crackers, bread, or fruit. For the ultimate indulgence, pair it with a glass of red wine, such as a full-bodied Cabernet Sauvignon.

6) Quality Assurance: Cote Hill Blue has received numerous accolades and awards, including being named a "superb blue crusted cheese" by experts. It has also undergone rigorous quality tests to ensure its exceptional taste and quality. As the Davenport family says, "We take great pride in our cheese and are confident you will taste the difference."
