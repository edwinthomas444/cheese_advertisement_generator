

1) Description of Cheese: Stinking Bishop, handmade by Charles Martell on his farm in Gloucestershire, England. This cheese is named after the variety of pear that is used to wash the cheese, giving it its distinctive nose.

2) Description of Product: Stinking Bishop is a soft textured washed rind cheese that develops a meaty pungency with age. It is typically the texture of thick clotted cream, but can sometimes be spoonable. Made from pasteurized cow's milk and using traditional rennet, this cheese is aged for at least 8 weeks.

3) Description of the Process: Stinking Bishop is carefully crafted by hand on Charles Martell's farm in Gloucestershire. The cheese is washed with the juice of the Stinking Bishop pear, giving it its unique flavor and aroma. It is then aged for a minimum of 8 weeks, allowing the flavors to develop and mature.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: Despite its strong and pungent aroma, Stinking Bishop has a delicate and herbaceous flavor. The meaty pungency develops with age, creating a complex and satisfying taste. The cheese is best enjoyed at room temperature to fully appreciate its unique flavors.

5) Serving Suggestions: To fully enjoy Stinking Bishop, serve it at room temperature and present it on a cheese board with crackers, bread, and fruit. It pairs well with a variety of foods, including cured meats, roasted vegetables, and figs. For a complete experience, pair it with a full-bodied red wine such as a Cabernet Sauvignon or a bold ale.

6) Quality Assurance: Stinking Bishop has received numerous accolades, including being featured in the Wallace & Gromit film "Return of the Wererabbit". It is also made with the highest quality ingredients and is carefully aged to perfection.
