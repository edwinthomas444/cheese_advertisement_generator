

1) Description of Cheese: Tunworth, handmade by Hampshire Cheeses in England, is a superb Camembert-style cheese with a rich history and geographical significance. Known as the Supreme Champion at the 2013 and 2006 British Cheese Awards, this cheese has also won a gold at the World Cheese Awards in 2007. It is highly regarded by renowned chef Raymond Blanc as the best "Camembert" in the world.

2) Description of Product: Tunworth is a pasteurised, bloomy English Camembert-style cheese, weighing 250g. It is handcrafted using special cultures and traditional rennet, and then aged for 4-6 weeks to achieve its unique and creamy flavor. Made from carefully selected cow's milk in the heart of Hampshire, this cheese has a medium strength and a soft, creamy texture.

3) Description of Process: The process of making Tunworth involves using special cultures and traditional rennet, and then maturing the cheese for 4-6 weeks. This careful preparation results in a stunning cheese with a rich and creamy flavor that is truly unique.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: Tunworth has a distinctive aroma, with hints of earthiness and a creamy, buttery scent. Its taste is equally delightful, with a rich and creamy texture and a unique flavor that is both savory and slightly tangy.

5) Serving Suggestions: For the best tasting experience, serve Tunworth at room temperature to allow its flavors to fully develop. It can be presented on a cheese board with crackers, bread, and fresh fruit. This cheese pairs well with a variety of foods, including cured meats, nuts, and honey. For a more indulgent experience, try pairing it with a glass of dry white wine.
