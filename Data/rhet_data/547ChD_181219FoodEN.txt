

1) Description of Cheese: Westcombe Cheddar, made by Lower Westcombe Farm in Evercreech, Somerset, is a traditional, hand-crafted cheddar that has been winning awards since 2018. It has also been recognized with European PDO status and Artisan Somerset Cheddar designation from Slow Food.

2) Description of Product: This cheddar is made with unpasteurised cow's milk and traditional rennet. It has a weight of 250g and boasts deep, nutty flavors with hints of hazelnut and citrusy, grassy notes. The cheese has a long, lingering finish with subtle caramel undertones.

3) Description of the Process: Westcombe Cheddar is made using traditional methods and is aged to perfection. The cheese is crafted by hand, resulting in a high-quality product that has been recognized with various awards.

4) Description of Smell and Taste: The cheese has a strong, yet pleasant aroma with notes of hazelnut and grass. Its taste is rich and complex, with a perfect balance of nutty and citrusy flavors. The lingering finish adds a touch of sweetness to the overall taste.

5) Serving Suggestions: For the best experience, we recommend serving Westcombe Cheddar at room temperature to allow its flavors to fully develop. It can be presented on a cheeseboard or enjoyed as part of a classic Ploughman's Lunch. This cheddar pairs well with a variety of beers, but a pale ale would be the perfect match.
