

1) Description of Cheese:
Suffolk Gold cheese, produced by Creeting St Mary in Suffolk, is a semi hard cheese with a rich history and geographical provenance. Its name is derived from the region it originates from, and is a testament to the quality and taste of this delicious cheese.

2) Description of Product:
This cheese is known for its semi-hard texture and golden hue, thanks to the use of Guernsey cow's milk. It is available in a convenient 250g size, making it perfect for sharing or enjoying on your own. The cheese is coated in an attractive rind, adding to its visual appeal. It is made using vegetarian rennet, making it suitable for vegetarians.

3) Description of Process:
Suffolk Gold cheese is made using traditional methods, with a special focus on the aging process. The use of Guernsey cow's milk gives it a creamy and buttery flavor, while the aging process adds depth and complexity to its taste. This cheese is carefully crafted to ensure the highest quality and flavor.

4) Description of Smell and Taste:
Upon opening, you will be greeted with a delightful aroma that is reminiscent of Caerphilly cheese. The taste is not dissimilar to cheddar, but with a slightly softer texture and a hint of sweetness from the high butterfat content. The rind is reminiscent of the French semi-hard cheese St Nectaire, adding a unique and enjoyable element to the overall taste.

5) Serving Suggestions:
For optimal enjoyment, we recommend serving Suffolk Gold cheese at room temperature. Its attractive rind and golden color make it a beautiful addition to any cheese board. Pair it with oatcakes and a crisp apple for a delicious snack or light meal. For the perfect beverage pairing, we suggest a red ale such as Rapture by Magic Rock Brewery.

6) Quality Assurance:
Suffolk Gold cheese has received numerous accolades and awards for its exceptional taste and quality. It is suitable for vegetarians and safe to eat during pregnancy, making it a versatile and guilt-free choice for all cheese lovers. Don't just take our word for it, try it for yourself and discover why this cheese is a favorite among cheese connoisseurs.
