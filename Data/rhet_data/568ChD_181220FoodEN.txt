

1) Description of Cheese:
Dorstone is a delicious British goats cheese made by the skilled cheese-maker, Charlie Westhead, in Hertfordshire. This cheese has a rich history, with its origins dating back to the ancient times when goats were first domesticated. The cheese is named after the picturesque village of Dorstone, located in the heart of England.

2) Description of Product:
Dorstone cheese is a unique and beautiful cheese, with its irregular cylinder shape, wrinkly geotrichum rind, and ash dusting. Each wheel weighs approximately 200g, making it the perfect size for sharing or indulging on your own. This cheese is made using traditional methods and is crafted from the finest unpasteurised goat's milk, giving it a distinct and bold flavor. The rennet used in the production of this cheese is also traditional, adding to its authenticity.

3) Description of Process:
The cheese-making process for Dorstone is a labor of love, with each wheel being carefully handcrafted by Charlie Westhead himself. The cheese is prepared using unpasteurised goat's milk, which is then aged for several weeks to develop its unique flavor and texture. During this time, the cheese is carefully monitored and turned to ensure even aging and the perfect balance of flavors.

4) Description of Smell and Taste:
Dorstone cheese has a distinct and inviting aroma, with hints of citrus and a subtle goaty tang. The taste is equally delightful, with a creamy and soft texture that melts in your mouth. The flavors are perfectly balanced, with the citrus notes complementing the slight tanginess of the goat's milk. This cheese is truly a treat for the senses.

5) Serving Suggestions:
To fully enjoy the flavors and textures of Dorstone cheese, we recommend serving it at room temperature. This will bring out the full depth of flavors and enhance the creamy texture. For presentation, we suggest pairing it with light buttermilk crackers and a cold white wine or pale ale. The citrus notes in the cheese make it a perfect accompaniment to a crisp white wine or a refreshing pale ale.
