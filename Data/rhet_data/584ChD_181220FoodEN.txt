

1) Description of Cheese:
Montgomery's Cheddar is a classic British cheddar produced by the Montgomery family at Manor Farm in Somerset, England. With a rich history dating back to the 1800s, this cheese is a true representation of traditional British cheddar-making.

2) Description of Product:
This cheddar is made from unpasteurized cow's milk and traditional rennet. It is matured for 12-18 months, resulting in a complex and nutty flavor. The cheese is available in a 250g block, making it perfect for snacking or incorporating into dishes.

3) Description of Process:
At Manor Farm, Montgomery's Cheddar is made seven days a week to ensure the freshness of the milk and the quality of the cheese. The milk comes from the farm's own herd of Friesian cows, ensuring a high-quality and authentic product.

4) Description of Smell and Taste:
The aging process of 12-18 months gives Montgomery's Cheddar a distinct and rich aroma. The cheese has a nutty and complex flavor, with a hint of sweetness and a sharp tang. The texture is smooth and creamy, making it a delight to eat.

5) Serving Suggestions:
For the best experience, serve Montgomery's Cheddar at room temperature. It can be enjoyed on its own or as part of a cheese platter. It pairs well with a hoppy IPA or a full-bodied red wine. For a classic British experience, try it in a ploughman's lunch.

6) Quality Assurance:
Montgomery's Cheddar has received numerous accolades, including winning the Best Traditional Cheddar at the World Cheese Awards. With its unpasteurized milk and traditional rennet, this cheese is a testament to the quality and craftsmanship of the Montgomery family. As quoted by cheese expert Juliet Harbutt, "Montgomery's Cheddar is simply the best cheddar in the world."
