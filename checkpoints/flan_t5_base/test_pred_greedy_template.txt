Craft Delights is a artisan cheese made from pasteurized milk by Dairy Haven Creameries, a cooperative cooperative cooperative cooperative cooperative cooperative in Wisconsin. It is the result of a long-term collaboration between the two companies, with a combined total of ninety-five percent of the production date being on time. The cheese is crafted at The Dairy Farm in the town of Wisconsin, using pasteurized milk and a heated, aging process.
###
This hard cheese is made from pasteurized cow's milk, with a smooth texture and a natural rind. It is available in a variety of sizes and weights, ranging from Grade A to Grade B. The cheese is coated in a natural color rind, adding to its unique appearance. Made with 45% water and culture, this cheese is suitable for vegetarians and is made using microbial rennet.
###
The cheese-making process begins with the curds being cut by hand and then transferred to the controlled environment. The curds are then moulded into square molds and pressed into molds with the resulting cheese. After being drained, the molds are left overnight and then masked with saline solution. The molds are then drained and rubbed up to remove excess moisture. The cheese is then aged for over a year, allowing it to develop its unique green rind and complex flavors.
###
The aroma of this cheese is earthy and aromatic, with hints of roasted pepper and a hint of salt. Upon tasting, you will experience a burst of flavors, starting with the rich and nutty notes of the milk, followed by the tangy notes of the cheese. The texture is crumbly and melts in the mouth, leaving a lingering taste that is both satisfying and addictive.
